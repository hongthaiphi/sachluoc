**Author:** Gisèle Pelicot | **Category:** Motivación e inspiración## ¿QUÉ ME APORTA ESTE TÍTULO? UNA HISTORIA DE VALENTÍA Y DIGNIDAD QUE CAMBIÓ EL SILENCIO POR JUSTICIA.

How do you survive your own story when you find out your life was the scene of a crime?

How do you face a court case where not only the attackers sit, but also prejudice, gossip, and sexism?

And what happens when a victim decides not to hide?

For almost ten years, a woman was raped in her own bed by many men without her knowing.

All this happened while she was asleep and fully drugged. How was this possible?

Her life partner, father of her children and grandfather of her grandchildren, arranged each attack through a website and sedated her so she could be abused by strangers.

This is the story of Gisèle Pelicot.

But here you will not find only the story of a terrible crime.

You will also know about a brave and strong decision by Gisèle: to refuse that shame falls on the victim.

In this Blink you will discover an unprecedented court case that shocked the world and started a global discussion about chemical submission, consent, and shared responsibility.

You will see how opening the trial to the public made this woman a symbol of active dignity.

This story is not easy to accept.

There are harsh images, nasty statements, and moments of unbelievable violence.

But it also tells about rebuilding after disaster.

Because as Gisèle said when leaving the court, shame must change sides.

A warning before you start: this Blink contains scenes of rape, violence, and murder. Please read carefully.

## UNA VIDA EN PAREJA QUE SE DERRUMBAN EN SEGUNDOS

On November 2, 2020, Gisèle and Dominique Pelicot must go to the police station in Carpentras, a city in southern France, about 25 kilometers from Avignon.

But why did the police call a retired couple with almost 50 years of marriage?

Two months before, a supermarket guard caught Dominique Pelicot filming under the skirts of three women with his phone.

The police questioned him and took his phone and computer.

This happened while Gisèle was away for a few days with her grandchildren.

When she came back, Dominique told her what happened. Gisèle was very angry: she told him to apologize to the women and promised to see a psychologist.

She said if it happened again, she would leave him.

He promised her it would not happen again.

That morning, on their way to the police station, Gisèle tried to calm Dominique: she thought the call was only a formality.

The officer in charge, Laurent Perret, let the husband go in first and then spoke with Gisèle alone.

He asked about her husband, whom she described as a good and kind man.

The police wanted to know if they had friends over, how social meetings were, what time she usually slept, and if she took naps.

Gisèle felt more confused when the officer asked if she and her husband practiced partner swapping.

She said no, she could not stand other men touching her because she did not see sex without love.

Then Perret told her he would show some pictures that she would not like.

The first photo showed a woman on a bed, deeply asleep, maybe dead.

A man was having sex with her.

Gisèle didn’t recognize the body, and she never wore a garter belt.

Perret asked her to look closely at the room.

It was her lamp and her bedside table.

But she didn’t remember anything and would never join something like that.

The police explained that if she could not remember that horror, it was because her husband drugged her to abuse her.

They told her that, according to what they found on Dominique’s computer, between 2011 and 2020, Gisèle was sedated and raped around 200 times by more than 50 men.

There were many photos and videos, but Gisèle asked not to see more.

From that moment, Pelicot was arrested.

A bomb had dropped.

Gisèle’s life fell apart.

She returned from the police station.

## UNA FAMILIA SE ASOMA AL ABISMO

Even though everything in her home looked the same, her house and the happy memories made with her family of 50 years were destroyed by what they found.

It seemed it was not a nightmare but the life she would face from now on.

Her first task was painful: to tell her children, David, Caroline, and Florian, about the tragedy revealed.

Their reactions went from shock to deep anger.

They wanted to protect her and destroy everything they found related to their father, in the house and in their hearts.

During that chaos, Florian’s wife remembered that years ago, Dominique told her grandson, David’s son, that they would play doctors.

The boy was three years old.

She never said anything because she doubted her own feelings.

Later, during another police search, they found signs of Dominique’s depravity: two photos of his daughter Caroline sleeping, showing an incestuous look.

They also found videos of his daughters-in-law in the shower, filmed with a tiny hidden camera.

If the family thought what happened to their mother was all, they were wrong.

From then on, the family was tortured by the doubt if the man had abused other members too.

Gisèle wanted to forget, as she had been forgetting many things for years.

One day she looked in the mirror and saw her hair was cut and dyed, but she did not remember going to the hairdresser.

Her birthday was yesterday, but she did not know how they celebrated.

She greeted a friend she had not seen for months, but the friend was surprised and said they had coffee last weekend.

Her forgetfulness and absences became so scary that she stopped driving.

Gisèle’s mother died from a brain tumor when Gisèle was nine years old.

Gisèle feared and obsessed about having the same cancer.

With her husband’s support, who knew her fears, she saw three neurologists.

No tumor or mental illness was found.

No one knew she had taken huge amounts of very strong sleeping pills without knowing, which damaged her memory badly.

Another strange symptom she told her husband was that she woke up with her pajamas very wet, like a woman about to give birth.

No gynecologist found a reason.

They never checked her for sexually transmitted diseases because she was an older married woman.

Especially with a man who always went to doctors with her and acted loyal and supportive.

Then she started hard medical tests to rule out sexual infections.

Months later, she learned one of the rapists had AIDS.

It was a miracle she did not get infected, because he abused her several times without using protection.

Gynecological exams never showed signs of forced sex because Dominique gave her a very strong muscle relaxant before the attacks.

Her hair held proof of the drugs her husband put in her food.

Her whole body changed from a victim of abuse to legal evidence.

## EL EXPEDIENTE CRECE Y TAMBIÉN EL HORROR

Almost a year passed.

The State collected evidence for the trial against the 54 accused men.

The court would try only 51, including Dominique Pelicot, because one fled and two died before the trial.

The press learned of the case and on October 6, 2021, the news came out.

Reading it left a bad taste: legally she was a victim, but she did not want to be one in life.

The press made her look pitiful, which she did not accept.

At the end of 2021, the case grew shocking.

About 40 attackers were arrested.

They discovered that Dominique used a sexual meeting website to contact men.

He showed photos of his wife to strangers interested.

One wanted to see Gisèle before deciding to rape her.

Dominique suggested pretending to sell bicycle wheels so the man could see her and decide.

The man lived nearby and greeted her many times after the crime like any neighbor.

Gisèle learned all rapists denied their crime and worse, said she agreed and took part in the orgy.

Every week, more rapists appeared.

Some were younger than Gisèle’s sons; up to 40 years younger.

The youth of these men was a mystery and extra pain for Gisèle.

When the news spread everywhere, she isolated herself and moved to the island of Ré at her daughter Caroline’s beach house.

She wanted to be alone.

But the case followed her: during 2018 holidays, her husband also arranged for her to be raped at that same house.

Videos showed an unknown man attacking her in her daughter’s room, wearing clothes Dominique had brought on the trip.

As the case went on, Gisèle found more details making the crime even worse.

The sick ritual started when Dominique took off his clothes and opened the door to the rapist.

The attacker had to strip and put his clothes in a neat pile by the door to avoid forgetting anything.

Once on the way back from the island, Gisèle didn’t remember if she had thrown out the trash.

Years later, she learned Dominique drugged her that day too to rape her in the car parking lot inside their car.

She saw the video of this in court.

Her husband could not help filming everything.

## LAS VÍCTIMAS HABLAN, PERO EL MACHISMO GRITA

In October 2022, a new terrible event happened.

Gisèle got a call from the police cold cases department.

Dominique Pelicot became the main suspect in the 1991 murder of Sophie Narme.

Sophie was a real estate agent who was drugged with ether, raped, strangled, and stabbed.

Pelicot denies the murder but was found guilty of an attempted rape in 1999, very similar to the 1991 crime.

Both women were real estate agents who met a man pretending to want to see a property, and then were attacked.

The 1999 victim escaped and went to the police right away.

Thanks to this, the rapist’s DNA was collected and 23 years later, Dominique was charged.

The woman identified him from the news images.

The case of Sophie is still open due to lack of proof.

Gisèle’s hell grew.

The man she lived with for two thirds of her life and built a family with had another victim and might be a murderer.

Meanwhile, some media voices said it was impossible for Madame Pelicot to be a victim.

They said she knew and agreed to everything.

How could she not remember?

Ten years and 200 rapes?

No, she cannot be innocent.

Even in the worst moments, sexism shows itself.

Gisèle started to see the helplessness victims of sexual abuse face.

If even with all the proof no one believes her, what happens when victims have no evidence society or law accept?

But Gisèle’s strength won: her anger became her power to heal.

The police had overwhelming proof: photos, videos, messages, and medical tests proving chemical submission.

Officers studied everything until feeling sick.

Gisèle didn’t need to prove anything; the evidence spoke clearly.

Still, she faced more than 45 defense lawyers and some attackers who said there was no rape because though she was asleep, they had her husband’s consent.

A week before the hearings started, she did not want a closed trial.

She imagined the trial as a wall of men against her, ready to say terrible things to break her down.

She feared their voices would drown hers and closing the doors would be a gift to them.

She would not protect the rapists from public eyes.

They should be ashamed, and she would keep her dignity.

As a victim, she had the right to decide: the trial would be public.

Her lawyers warned her that she would have to see all the evidence first, as it would be bad to see that horror for the first time in court.

She had to be brave: until then, she had only seen the three photos from 2020.

She opened the first link her lawyers sent.

There was that lifeless body, tied hands and feet.

She could be a corpse but was snoring loudly.

The husband and the attacker opened her mouth.

She choked and almost drowned, but they kept going.

Watching, she felt that, although it happened to her, she wasn’t that lifeless body.

She was alive and in control of what was coming.

## LA VERGÜENZA DEBE CAMBIAR DE BANDO

At the start of September 2024, the criminal court of Avignon opened the trial.

The accused looked defiant; they wanted to say they were innocent.

No one knew Gisèle would ask for the courtroom doors to be open.

When a lawyer announced this, a heavy silence filled the room.

The court president was angry about the decision, which broke traditions but not the law.

The attackers’ faces changed.

They started shouting, asking for a closed trial.

Even the prosecutors asked for that, surprising Gisèle.

But protests did not change the law, and the right the victim asked for was granted reluctantly.

So began one of the most covered trials in recent history.

During the months of the trial, Gisèle heard many insults.

The judge asked a doctor if her fluids showed pleasure.

One attacker said if he wanted to rape, he would have chosen a younger prettier woman.

Days later, women and men came to court to support Gisèle.

Day after day, people greeted her with applause, and the public gallery filled.

It was like this for all four months of the trial.

The press covered the story worldwide.

Her story became huge.

Meanwhile, she wanted to give back dignity to her tortured body and everything that rape tried to destroy.

About Dominique, the psychiatrist said he had two personalities: one linked to reality and one to fantasies.

The report described him as a man without empathy and a perverse narcissist with almost all known sexual disorders.

All accused were found guilty.

Pelicot got the maximum sentence: 20 years in prison.

The sentence included the crimes against his daughter and daughters-in-law.

On the last morning of the trial, Gisèle got a call from officer Laurent Perret, who in 2020 questioned her and showed her the photos.

He said he would be honored to walk her out of the court.

Her first words to the press were for her children, grandchildren, and daughters-in-law, whose lives were also broken.

Then she mentioned the unknown victims whose pain stayed with her during the trial.

Finally, she said that now society must think and change.

A giant banner in Avignon reads: Thank you, Gisèle.

In this Blink about A hymn to life, by Gisèle Pelicot, you have seen the story from the arrest, trial, and sentence to Dominique Pelicot and the more than 50 rapists who took part in the evil attacks.

## RESUMEN FINAL

You have also learned that domestic violence and chemical submission are realities we must know and report.

But this is not just the story of a bad man. Even after being a victim of unimaginable betrayal and years of hidden sexual violence, Gisèle showed it is possible to change pain into strength and dignity.

As she says, shame must change sides, from the victim to the attacker, because telling the truth brings justice and wakes society’s conscience.
