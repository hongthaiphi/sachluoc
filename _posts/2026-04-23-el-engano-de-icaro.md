**Author:** Seth Godin | **Category:** Motivación e inspiración## ¿QUÉ ME APORTA ESTE TÍTULO? UN CÓDIGO CREATIVO PARA ACTIVAR TU ARTISTA INTERIOR.

The digital revolution is here to stay.

Computers are getting smarter every day.

Jobs that are boring, repetitive, and safe, like the usual nine-to-five routine, are disappearing.

This is not necessarily bad, but it means you must adapt to the new digital economy.

So, how do you do that?

Well, you need to embrace creativity and become an artist.

As Seth Godin says, art is not just about painting pictures or composing music. It includes any creative work that needs passion and smart ideas.

In other words, things computers just cannot do.

Being an artist is not easy.

It needs full dedication and strong courage.

It is also risky: unlike old office jobs, there is no fixed salary for your effort.

But it is more rewarding and better for society as a whole.

After all, who would not want to live in a world where people follow what really matters to them?

But you don’t have to do it alone.

These summaries end common myths and give many tips on how to unlock your inner creative potential.

Do you want to learn how to use your creativity to stand out from machines and secure your future in the new digital economy?

## EL ENGAÑO DE ÍCARO NOS HACE SER DEMASIADO CAUTELOSOS, PERO NECESITAMOS SALIR DE NUESTRA ZONA DE CONFORT.

Let’s get started.

The future will be more and more automated.

Machines will do more manual and office tasks.

So, what is left for humans?

We will need to be much more creative.

But we took too long to realize this.

The blame is on the Icarus myth.

We live in a society that accepts too little.

We took the Greek myth of Icarus very seriously, but also misunderstood it.

Icarus was the son of Daedalus, an artisan jailed with his son for disobeying King Minos of Crete.

But Daedalus had a plan and built wings for himself and Icarus to escape.

He told his son not to fly too high so the sun would not melt the wax holding the wings.

Icarus ignored him and flew toward the sun.

As expected, his wings melted and he fell.

Our culture focuses on this part.

“Don’t fly too high!”

That is the lesson we learned.

But there is more.

Daedalus also warned Icarus not to fly too low, because sea splashes would wet the wings and pull him down.

Aiming high can be risky, but being too careful is risky too.

It is worth remembering this second advice.

If we want to thrive, we have to leave our comfort zone, which means not flying too low.

The world has changed.

In the past, getting a safe and well-paid office job with benefits was possible.

That model ended in the 1990s.

Today, only creative people who take risks and start new projects have stable jobs.

Think of Internet startups like Facebook, launched when this business model was not sure to succeed.

We need to learn from this.

The new comfort zone means creativity and connecting with others.

## EN LA NUEVA ECONOMÍA CONECTADA HAY MENOS GUARDIANES Y MÁS OPORTUNIDADES.

When a kitten is in danger, its mom or dad picks it up by the neck and takes it to safety.

A monkey, however, must hold tightly to its mom or dad’s back to escape danger.

In the new economy, humans must be more like monkeys and less like kittens.

We must be more active.

So, what defines the new economy?

Two things stand out.

It is more connected and there are fewer gatekeepers.

These two things are linked.

As everyone connects through the Internet, gatekeepers find it harder to tell us what to do or limit us.

For example, creative jobs.

Musicians or actors once needed approval from industry leaders, like agents or directors, the gatekeepers.

Today, people can take control of their careers.

Platforms like YouTube and iTunes let anyone reach their target audience and share creative work.

This creates many new chances.

Let’s think about the music industry.

Before the Internet, the path to success was clear.

You waited for a record label to find you, wrote a hit song, and hoped bosses did not cheat you.

Chances to succeed as a musician were small.

Today, none of that applies.

Write and produce a song that only two people buy on iTunes, and you earn more than old royalty payments for selling a whole album.

But it is not just musicians who benefit from the new connected economy.

Designers, consultants, teachers, and therapists can build online businesses on YouTube, websites, and social media to reach millions.

## NECESITAMOS REDEFINIR LA HUMILDAD Y COMPROMETERNOS PLENAMENTE CON NUESTRO ARTE.

There is a Japanese word for fully committing to an activity and getting deeply involved: kamiwaza.

It literally means “like the gods.”

It may sound very arrogant, but this is because we have the wrong idea about humility and need to change it.

Humility does not mean accepting poor work or not trying your best.

On the contrary, there is humility in working hard to be the best you can.

When you fully commit to a task, you immerse yourself and stop thinking about what you do.

This means you stop worrying about what others think.

This is true humility.

When you reach this kind of humility, you work better with and for others, sharing your best and your achievements.

It lets you take responsibility and lead while taking risks.

For example, if you love fashion, it is more humble to start your own fashion brand than to stay in an uninspired office job.

Redefining humility this way helps you focus on giving your best to society without arrogance or selfishness.

And another point about humility: it is needed to fully commit to your art.

Commitment is the key to success: if you keep your job safe and only work on your art or passion part-time, you are unlikely to achieve much.

Creating something to show the world and inspire people needs humility and effort.

But success is not guaranteed.

Unlike a regular job, art has no fixed salary.

All your hard work may lead to nothing.

This is a tough truth, and accepting it needs both confidence and humility.

## PARA CONVERTIRTE EN ARTISTA, TIENES QUE SER INDEPENDIENTE Y DESARROLLAR DETERMINACIÓN.

What is determination?

It is not something you might find in salad leaves!

Actually, it is one of the most important qualities an artist must have.

To be clear, if you want to be an artist, you must have determination.

Art is not just painting or making music.

It is any creative activity you do to bring something new into the world.

It might be a new way to help customers or a fresh style of abstract painting.

Whatever it is, you will need determination to complete it.

Listen to psychologist Angela Duckworth, author of Grit.

Many people think determination only means perseverance.

But they are not the same.

Determination means setting clear goals that reflect your true passions.

For example, creating a social platform for environmental activists.

The vision is important.

Once you know what you want, it is easier to keep trying to reach it.

But determination is not the only trait you need in the new economy; you also need to be independent in mind and spirit.

Outside control stops your dreams.

To be a true artist, you must avoid influences like bosses who control your work.

Independence also means not relying on outside motivation.

To succeed, you must be self-sufficient.

How you feel about yourself and your work should not depend on others’ approval.

You are the only judge of your work.

Finally, you must become indifferent to success and failure.

Turn on TV and you see many successful artists making music that soon is forgotten.

Fame does not always show the true value of what you create.

Remember, not getting big recognition does not mean your work is not great and deep.

## LA VERGÜENZA ES EL MAYOR ENEMIGO DEL ARTISTA, ASÍ QUE ES MEJOR ENFOCARSE EN LA CONEXIÓN E IGNORAR LAS CRÍTICAS.

Superheroes can do almost anything, but they often have a weak spot.

Think of Superman and kryptonite, the alien mineral that takes away his powers instantly.

Artists have their kryptonite too. It is less exotic: shame.

Why are artists so vulnerable to shame?

It is because they give their full effort to their creative work.

Their work is very personal and very important to them.

That is why criticism hurts so much.

Imagine your boss criticizes your note-taking skills during meetings.

You probably would not care much, right?

But imagine how much a criticism hurts when aimed at a startup plan you have worked on for months and believe in deeply.

Criticism is often a way to shame people.

And shame has long been used to make people follow society’s rules.

Teachers, for example, often try to control independent students who think differently by shaming them in front of classmates.

Society also stops us from following our dreams and being free by making us feel ashamed of what it sees as arrogance or nonsense.

This means you have to get stronger and learn to ignore criticism if you want to succeed as an artist.

Because shame will stop your efforts quickly.

Your only choice is to not let others’ opinions distract you from your goals.

What is the best way to do this?

Focus on positive connections and avoid looking for negative comments.

After all, just one negative comment can make you doubt yourself.

## PARA TENER MÁS ÉXITO, APRENDE A RECONOCER LAS OPORTUNIDADES SIENDO MÁS OBSERVADOR.

Author Ray Bradbury once gave good advice to creative beginners.

He said the most important thing is to avoid getting stuck in your own mind.

Being creative means interacting with the world, not only thinking about it.

If you want to interact, you must really learn to look at the world.

Why is this important?

Well, success means seeing opportunities.

But we usually see the world only through our narrow view.

We interpret things based on what we already believe, which clouds our judgment.

Think about how many people see new technologies.

They worry these will make the world worse and choose to ignore them.

In the end, they miss many great chances.

Ask Fred Wilson, a venture capitalist who made a fortune by seeing the world as it really is.

Knowing where things were going, he invested in Internet platforms such as Twitter, which became a big success.

Publishers Alan Webber and Bill Taylor are another example.

They saw that Internet startups were the business of the future and started Fast Company, a magazine about this new sector, in 1995.

It became very successful.

These pioneers all share the ability to recognize opportunities when they see them.

But this skill is not something they were born with.

They learned it by training themselves to be more observant.

Small things really can make a difference.

For example, author Paco Underhill is known for his attention to detail.

During a consulting job for a retail company, he noticed female customers disliked being brushed by others while shopping.

He recommended stores widen their aisles.

What happened?

A big increase in profits!

## LOS VERDADEROS ARTISTAS SE CARACTERIZAN POR SU OBSESIÓN CON SU ARTE Y NO POR SU ESTATUS SOCIAL.

Steve Martin is one of the world’s most successful comedians, but actually, he is not that funny.

How is that possible?

Well, art is more about obsession than talent.

Martin is a classic example.

In a way, he is an “anti-comedian”: he is not very expressive and not famous for hilarious jokes.

This is why he spent a long time failing in his career, often traveling across the country to perform for just three or four people.

But he did not let that stop him.

He kept working and obsessed over small details, like the perfect hand gesture or the right time to end a sketch.

In the end, his work paid off.

Because of his care for comedy details, his not-so-funny side began to entertain a growing audience.

He was very funny because he was not a usual comedian.

The audience began cheering, urging him to finish sketches and finishing his lines for him.

Gradually, he stopped being just a comedian and became a host and entertainer.

This shows being an artist does not always fit common stereotypes.

Another wrong idea is that you must be a poor, troubled soul to succeed as an artist.

This image shows the artist as a wild bohemian, dressed in rags, who challenges usual morals.

But like the mistaken idea about talent, this is a myth.

Think about TED talks.

Most speakers are perfectly normal people, not eccentric.

Sometimes, they are even a bit boring.

What makes them special is their full dedication to making a difference in their fields.

And that is the sign of a true artist.

Writers often talk about being “blocked.”

## ESCRIBIR TODOS LOS DÍAS TE HARÁ MÁS CREATIVO, PERO RECUERDA SER UN JEFE AMABLE CONTIGO MISMO.

But have you noticed almost no one suffers from “speaker’s block”?

Talking is so natural that it is hard to imagine being unable to find words.

After all, we get up every day, see people, and talk with them.

Writing, however, is totally different.

Putting your thoughts on paper is often the bottleneck that stops your creativity.

The key is to treat writing like talking.

That means doing it every day!

You do not worry about talking because you do it all the time automatically.

If you make a habit of writing regularly, a blank page will soon be less scary.

Your writing will become like speaking: you will find a flow and your own unique voice.

As they say, practice makes perfect.

So make a rule to sit down once a day and write about something important to you.

Even better, share it with the world by posting on a blog.

This idea applies to any goal.

If you want to be a filmmaker but feel stuck each time you hold a camera, develop the habit of filming every day.

Do it enough, and it will become natural.

This needs self-discipline, but remember to be a kind boss to yourself.

Being your own boss is often harder than having one.

There are fewer limits, and you may become much harder on yourself than anyone else.

The best way to avoid this is to watch how you talk to yourself.

Try to be constructive and encouraging, not just critical.

Point out what you have achieved and praise yourself, even if it is something small.

And most importantly, stop telling yourself you cannot do something!

Remember Steve Martin from earlier?

This is the attitude to take.

Who cares if you are not perfect yet?

The only thing that will help you improve is chasing your passion without stopping.

Once you start doing this, you will be ready to thrive in today’s changing world!

## RESUMEN FINAL

Art is about creativity, and this applies to any field.

Whether you want to be a painter or open a new online boutique, creative thinking is what gives you an edge in today’s fast digital economy.

Computers can do routine work, but there is plenty of space for independent thinkers and creators full of great ideas and passion to realize them.

So stop worrying about instant success or others’ approval and jump in!

Remember art will not always be fun.

Whatever you want to create, there will be hard times, challenges, and even pain.

But don’t give up.

These are the obstacles every artist faces on their path.

Accept them and focus on how much hardship you can bear.

The main question to ask yourself is if the final product is really worth it (it usually is).

This will help you accept pain as an inevitable part of your life as an artist.

Once you understand this, you are much less likely to quit your project.
