### Synopsis

*One Move Makes All the Difference* (2025) looks at how small, planned steps can help you get back control of your thoughts, feelings, and actions. It uses ideas from brain science, psychology, and true stories. It shows you the TEAM framework – Thoughts, Emotions, Actions, and Manifestation. This framework gives you a clear way to stop negative thinking, feel less tired, and make lasting changes in your work and life that fit what is important to you.

## What will you get from this? Use your TEAM to change your thoughts, actions, and future.

The blanket scratches your skin when you wake up. You are sweating before the sun is even high. You get up from the cold floor. You take three slow steps to a bucket in the corner. You look through the barred window. You see only a small yard with high walls around it. A tray makes a noise at your door. It's the same gray food that tastes like nothing. Soon, you will stand in the bright sun. You will break stones into small pieces to build a road. Maybe you will never walk on that road.

Nelson Mandela lived like this for 18 years of his 27 years in prison. He was on Robben Island. He had a small cell, seven by nine feet. He had to do hard work. There were long times of silence. Even his own thoughts could be dangerous then. But he did not let his mind become another prison. He kept his goal in mind. He found freedom even when there was no freedom outside.

You might not be in prison. But maybe you feel like that. You feel stuck. You have a quiet pain, even if your life seems okay. You have a dream you forgot. Or a goal you cannot reach. Your life might look good, but you do not feel free.

This is what this summary is about. Here, you will learn how your thoughts create your life. You will learn how to stop trapping yourself. You will find out how to stop the old ways of thinking that keep you stuck. You will learn to see failure differently. You will learn how to move forward even when you feel no desire to do anything. True freedom begins in your mind. Change starts when you believe that something else can happen.

## Blink 1 - How you think can trap you — or set you free

You have built a careful life. You have a stable job, a nice home, and good money. But does it sometimes feel like it is holding you back? You have done what you thought you should do. Still, something bothers you. Maybe it's a dream you forgot. Or a different way of life you thought about but never tried.

This feeling is not by chance. You have built it with each thought and each choice. Research from Queen’s University shows you have more than 6,000 thoughts each day. But there is a problem. Your brain does not just* lean* toward the negative – it actively looks for them. 

This is called *frequency bias*. When you notice one negative thing, you start to see negative things everywhere. If you only look at what is wrong in your life, you will suddenly see problems blocking your way. It is not that more problems appeared. You have just taught your brain to look for them.

What is the worst part? You might be scared of what other people would think if you tried to dream in a new way. Numbers from the University of Scranton show that most people do not reach their New Year’s goals. About 92 out of 100 people fail. But it is not about wanting it enough. What truly makes a change is how you think and how hopeful you are about what can happen.

So, you might ask, how can you change your way of thinking? If you have trapped yourself in your mind, you can also free yourself. If you believe you can break free, you will. The author saw this happen to himself. When he was 35, a hand injury ended his job as a dentist. He could not even open a packet of sugar without pain. He realized something very hard: his whole sense of self came from his job. But then, his husband helped him. This support changed his thinking from “I will never be okay” to “I will be okay.”

That one change in thought led to a new job in Florida. It took 17 months of doing something every day. Each day meant taking one step. He made a phone call, sent a CV, or contacted someone. Even on days when people said no, just moving forward helped. One 'yes' was enough to start a totally new life.

Think of it this way: the future has not happened yet. So, a good result is just as possible as a bad one. You choose what kind of life you are working for. You change by understanding what the author calls *TEAM*. This means *Thoughts* – where everything starts. Then, *Emotions* – the feelings your thoughts make. Next are *Actions* – what you do because of your thoughts and feelings. And finally, *Manifestations* – the things you make happen in your life.

In the next parts, you will learn how to use each part of your TEAM. This will help you get unstuck. Change starts in your head. The first step is to decide that a different life is possible.

## Blink 2 - Thoughts are the captain of your TEAM

Think about when you were a child. Did people call you names? Like 'lazy' or 'outsider'? Those words stayed with you. Even now, many years later, you can still hear them in your head. Mendelson says we are like gummy bears. We have sticky surfaces. Every bad comment sticks to us and becomes part of who we think we are.

This is the T in TEAM – your *thoughts*. Your thoughts are like the captain. They lead everything that happens next. Your thoughts turn into your beliefs. And your beliefs make you who you are and how you live in the world. 

It is dangerous when you start to believe these labels. They stop you from reaching your full ability. If you call yourself a 'fraud' or think you 'don't belong,' you will feel scared and unsure. This makes you less likely to try for big projects or new chances. 

In fact, studies on imposter syndrome show that it affects very successful people the most. It traps them in a pattern. They either prepare too much or put things off. Then, they cannot accept their successes, even when they do very well.

The good news is you can stop this pattern. Use what the author calls* NBC-A*: Notice, Breathe, Choose, Act. When you start to feel strong emotions, notice them. Take a breath to give yourself a moment. Choose how to react by 'testing the reality' of the situation. Ask yourself: Was the danger real? Did it truly hurt you? Is your reaction too big for what happened? Then, act based on that clear way of thinking.

To 'test reality' means to ask questions about your thoughts without bias. Was what that person did done on purpose? Did it really hurt you? Most of the time, people act without thinking much. They don't mean to be bad. The driver who drove in front of you likely did not see you. They were not trying to get you.

Here, we look at Dr. Carol Dweck’s work on 'growth mindset' and 'fixed mindset.' This shows how powerful it is to believe you can get better. People with a 'growth mindset' see mistakes as a way to learn. They do not think mistakes show if they are good or bad people. They know that with hard work and practice, they can improve. People with a 'fixed mindset' believe their skills cannot change. They think you are either smart or not, good at sports or not.

But remember this: The IQ test was not made to measure how smart you are for good. Its creator wanted to find children who needed extra help in school. This was so they could learn better. Being smart is not something you are born with. It grows over time with effort and practice. You just need to believe you can improve, and then you will.

When you control your thoughts and think about good things that can happen, you can create any future you want.

## Blink 3 - Your worst days prove how resilient you are

Let's think back for a moment to the author's birthday in Cancun in 2021. To put it simply, he got food poisoning. Then, there was shooting near the pool, and he had to hide in his hotel room. For 45 minutes, he did not know if his husband was safe. The fear of that day was extreme. He heard shots, saw people running and screaming outside his window. He called his husband. Then he thought the ringing phone might tell a gunman where he was hiding. This is still one of the worst things that ever happened to him.

But he survived that day. You have also survived every hard thing you have gone through. When bad feelings take over, just knowing this can make you strong. Your worst days show how strong you truly are.

This brings us to E in TEAM – *emotions*. To understand your feelings, you need to start with your brain. The front part of your brain helps you think, plan, and use logic. But if your brain thinks there is danger, another part, called the amygdala, can take over. This part controls feelings and reactions. When it takes over, you cannot think clearly. You react in very strong, often too-big ways. That is why stopping for a moment is important. Taking a breath or walking outside for a bit calms your amygdala. It lets the front of your brain take back control.

But your emotions are not your enemies. Think of them like unusual friends. They affect you in certain ways. Studies show that anger and feeling embarrassed stop you from controlling yourself. They make you take more risks. Sadness makes you expect less. Anxiety makes bad things seem more likely than good things. Knowing these patterns helps you see when your feelings are making you choose things you will wish you hadn't later.

The answer is not to hide your feelings. It is also not to use 'toxic positivity.' That means thinking only 'good vibes' and pushing away all bad feelings. In fact, studies show that holding in negative feelings can make you more stressed. It can hurt your heart. It can also make anxiety and sadness worse. If you do not let yourself feel bad emotions, they become stronger. They then control your experiences more.

One of the best ways to grow good feelings is to keep a gratitude journal. Each night for a week, write down three good things. Also, write why they were good. This has good effects that last for six months. It can even help the bodies of people with heart problems. When you often think about what you are thankful for, it changes how you react emotionally. It helps you feel better overall.

## Blink 4 - Action is the only way to move forward

In 1958, a high school student named Bob Heft made a new design for the American flag. It was for a history project. He added stars for Alaska and Hawaii because he heard they would become states. His teacher gave him a B minus. Bob could have just taken that grade and forgotten about it. But he asked what he could do to get a better grade. The teacher said if he could get Washington to use his flag design, he would think about changing the grade.

Bob made it happen. It took him two years. He sent eight letters to the White House. He made 21 phone calls to people far away. But President Eisenhower liked the design. He chose it to be the official flag. Bob went back to his teacher and got his A.

Bob’s story shows how powerful action is. Action is the A in TEAM. Action is the simplest of the four TEAM steps. You either do something, or you stay still. That’s all. You can train your mind from the inside. This means starting with your thoughts and feelings. But you can also train it from the outside. You can take action first, see what happens, and use that good feeling to keep going.

But how do you even start? This is where 'finding your candy' helps. This means finding something that gives you a quick good feeling. This good feeling then makes it easier to act. Studies on doctors showed that those who got candy before checking patients did better than those who did not. Thinking about a small reward made their brains work better. Your 'candy' could be thinking about your dream future. It could be saying a positive phrase. Or it could be the good feeling you get after finishing a small task.

Once you have your 'candy,' remember to start small. Make your bed in the morning. Studies show this simple act makes you feel good about what you have done. It can also help you be more productive all day. Research also shows that tidy places lower stress and help you make better choices. When you do one thing and feel good, you use that good feeling to power your next action.

Break your big dreams into smaller projects and tasks. A project needs many steps to finish, like starting a dog grooming business. A task is something you can do right away. You do not need to break it down more. For example, looking for lawyers or making a meeting. Put one task for your project on your daily to-do list. Finish it. Then do the next easiest thing, and then the next. Each action helps the next one. This builds a strong push that helps you move forward, even when your thoughts and feelings are not working together.

## Blink 5 - Why happiness is more like weather than a finish line

The author was making his life new after he became sick and could not work. His husband gave him a gift: a magic wand from their acupuncturist's wall. The card said, “I am very proud of you. I want you to know this really works when you believe in yourself.” The magic was not in the wand itself. It was in Mendelson’s wish to work hard and long to make his dream real.

This is the M in TEAM – manifestation. This means putting your thoughts, feelings, and actions together. You do this to make real things happen.

But there is a small problem: making your dream happen will not make you happy forever. Taylor Swift won Album of the Year at the Grammys in 2016. She then asked herself, “What now?” She had reached her top goal. She saw that winning awards did not keep her happy for long. By 2024, when she won again, she understood something new: “For me, the award is the work.” She found happiness in making songs, getting ready for shows, and doing what she loves. Not just in getting prizes.

This is called *hedonic adaptation*. Studies show that people who win the lottery feel very happy at first. But then, they go back to how happy they were before they won. Getting something good gives you a short feeling of joy. But you always go back to your normal level of feeling. That is why if you link your happiness to reaching goals, you will often feel let down.

It means you cannot feel happy all the time and push away sadness, anger, and other bad feelings. Happiness comes and goes. Sometimes it just disappears for no clear reason. And if you try hard to catch it, thinking it will fix everything, you often find it when you are not looking for it.

So, do not see your new dream as the answer to being unhappy. Instead, change your thinking from an end goal to a journey. Life is something to *live, *not to *finish*. Making things happen and feeling happy create a cycle that keeps itself going. Living and creating bring happiness. This happiness then helps you create and live more. If you notice you are not acting or feeling as you wish, do the one thing that will help you start again.

This is where Dr. Tal Ben-Shahar’s SPIRE model helps. It is a plan for being well in all parts of your life, not just for reaching goals. SPIRE means Spiritual, Physical, Intellectual, Relational, and Emotional well-being. When you work on each part of SPIRE, you become well in every way. Real happiness needs you to care for all parts of your life. Not just change one thing. The happiness you want comes from choosing every day to work on each area. It comes from being proud of the work itself. And it comes from continuing on whatever path life shows you.

## Final summary

In this summary of *One Move Makes All the Difference* by Martin R. Mendelson, you have learned about your inner TEAM. Your TEAM – your thoughts, feelings, actions, and things you make happen – can either keep you stuck or make you free. 

You learn to notice and question thoughts that hold you back. You let yourself feel emotions but do not let them control you. You break big dreams into small, easy actions. This way, you start to create a life that truly fits who you are. Happiness is not a finish line. It is more like the weather, always changing. It comes naturally when you keep working on yourself, one step at a time.

Life talks to you if you are ready to listen. Be open to all things that can happen. Every choice takes you to the next path. Understand how things connect as you go. Trust that where you are now is the right place to be for your next steps.
