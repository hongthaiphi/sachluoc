**Author:** J. Chad Mitchell | **Category:** Motivation & Inspiration## WHAT’S IN IT FOR ME? BE THE LEADER THE WORLD NEEDS.

The world has a leadership problem, but it is not what you think.

We do not need more leaders – we need leaders who are worth following.

Look at any newspaper and you will see politicians who want money, business leaders who only care about short-term profits, and influencers who spread empty values.

The older generation gave us a world full of problems, and now they give advice based on the same mistakes.

Here is what they will not tell you: the best leaders for young people are not old executives or experienced politicians.

They are people like you.

Right now, there are 1.

8 billion people between 10 and 24 years old – the largest young group ever.

They do not want advice from the generation that caused the problems.

They want leaders who are their peers and understand what they face.

This is a great chance for you.

This Blink will show you how to take it.

You will learn why your choices are important for you and others.

You will also learn clear rules to help you become a good leader and the challenges you may meet.

So, are you ready to start leading today?

Let’s begin!

## YOUR INVISIBLE IMPACT

You may have thought: What I do does not matter.

One person cannot change things.

Your choices are small in a big world with many problems.

This way of thinking is harmful and wrong.

The author Chad Mitchell’s daughter, Chloe, was moving to college in Virginia.

She cried the whole car trip, afraid and unsure about a new place.

When they arrived, a teammate named Lauren welcomed Chloe with a hug and smile.

That small moment changed everything.

The kindness helped Chloe feel less afraid and helped her do well at school.

Years later, Mitchell thanked Lauren for helping Chloe.

Lauren did not remember this at all.

It was so simple and natural to her that she forgot it.

But it changed Chloe’s whole experience.

This is the secret of leadership: your biggest effects are often invisible to you.

You cannot see how far your actions reach.

A kind word can stop someone from making a big mistake.

Your honesty could inspire someone to change their life.

You really have the power to do good.

## THE INNER WOLVES YOU FEED

An old story shows how leadership works.

A grandfather talks to his grandson, who is angry because he was bullied.

The grandfather says he feels like two wolves live inside him.

One is the White Wolf, who is good and peaceful.

The other is the Red Wolf, full of anger and ready to fight.

The grandson asks which wolf wins.

The grandfather says: “Whichever one I feed.”

This means what you focus on grows.

What you practice gets stronger.

If you use your energy for kindness, care, and responsibility – your Leadership Wolf – these qualities will grow.

If you feed bad habits like rudeness or not caring – your Red Wolf – those grow instead.

You are not fixed.

You are the result of your choices.

Leadership is not something you are born with.

It is a choice you make many times when no one is looking.

Think about a penny that doubles every day.

Start with one cent today.

Tomorrow it is two cents.

Keep doubling for one month, and you get over five million dollars.

This is the power of compounding, and it is not just about money.

It works for habits too.

Eat healthy breakfast daily and see your health and energy improve over time.

Be trustworthy all the time and watch your friendships grow.

Small good habits done regularly create big change.

The real question is: are you growing your White Wolf or your Red Wolf?

## THE ELUSIVE AVERAGE

After WWII, the US Air Force wanted a new cockpit design.

They looked for an “average pilot” to build it for.

But from over 4,000 pilots, not one was average in all ways!

No pilot was truly average.

The solution was smart: adjustable seats and straps for every pilot.

This saved many lives by accepting a simple truth: no one is standard.

You are not average.

Not in your body, your story, or your view of the world.

And no one else is average either.

There has never been anyone just like you, and there never will be again.

Your mix of background, skills, challenges, and ideas is one of a kind.

This is very important for leadership.

One way of leading does not fit all people.

People are not replaceable parts.

A leader who acts as if everyone is the same misses the point.

Great leaders see what makes each person special.

They find what each person does best and help them use it.

This is how you build strong teams.

Not by making everyone the same, but by helping everyone feel they belong.

When people are seen and valued for who they are, they don’t just join – they care deeply.

Feeling you belong helps people do great things together.

Sometimes pride makes us ignore what makes others different.

We think our way is the only way.

Worse, some leaders use these differences to divide people and keep power for themselves.

This is the dark side of leadership – the Red Wolf using division to win.

## THE SKILL THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING

Studies show that about two-thirds of young adults feel very lonely.

Think about that.

In the most connected time in history, loneliness is common.

What do these lonely people need?

They need the same thing everyone needs: to feel truly cared for.

Even a simple “How are you?” with real care can change someone’s day.

This is true for everyone, from classmates to stars playing sports worldwide.

This is not just advice – it is the base of real leadership.

At its heart is empathetic listening.

Empathy means understanding how someone feels and what they are going through.

Listening with empathy shows that you care and respect their experience.

The speaker feels valued.

The listener learns more deeply.

Both feel closer.

Here is how to do it.

First, put away your phone.

Completely.

Give your full attention.

Second, listen with your whole body.

Face the person.

Keep your arms open.

Make eye contact.

Your body talks as much as your words.

Third, focus on what they say, not on your answer.

Do not plan your reply while they speak.

Finally, do not rush to fix their problem.

When someone shares a struggle, we often want to give solutions fast.

Do not do this.

Trying to fix quickly takes away their chance to solve problems themselves.

It also stops you from truly understanding them.

If you want to show care, try to understand first.

This is what great leaders do.

They try to understand people, not judge them.

Judgment means focusing on what someone does and saying that is who they are.

You put them into a box.

Understanding means focusing on how they feel at the moment.

You see them as changeable and strong.

This difference matters a lot.

Judgment closes doors.

Understanding opens them.

One feeds your Red Wolf.

The other feeds your Leadership Wolf.

## STAYING OUT OF THE SHADE

Have you ever seen someone become better from criticism and insults?

Mitchell has not.

But many people choose Team Shade – judging, blaming, and using others to gain for themselves.

They say bad things and wonder why no one follows them.

Team Sun is different.

These people lift others with their words.

They help, not use.

They look for the good and make it bigger.

Team Sun feeds your White Wolf.

Team Shade feeds your Red Wolf.

Decide now: Which team will you join?

Being Team Sun is not easy.

Life is hard.

You will make mistakes.

Sometimes you do not know how to help.

But you can choose never to be Team Shade.

You can choose to see the good in others – you will always find some.

You can practice empathetic listening and trying to understand.

Team Sun means putting others first.

The best leaders in history were driven by love for others.

Think of Jesus Christ.

Or Buddha, who said one flame can light many candles without losing any fire.

Giving love is not a limited resource.

It is endless.

This is a surprising truth about leadership: when you give to others, you do not lose yourself.

You grow.

The more you give, the more you have to give.

Your power to make a difference gets bigger.

Start today.

Find one person who needs support and give it sincerely.

See what happens – to them and to you.

## THE “-ISMS” THAT KILL LEADERSHIP

There are many rules for being a good leader.

We talked about many.

But there are also many problems and distractions.

The biggest are what Mitchell calls “the Four ‘-isms’ of the Leadership Apocalypse”: tribalism, racism, sexism, and materialism.

Tribalism means treating groups as less because they are not yours.

US politics is full of this.

Leaders use loyalty to their group and hatred of others to gain power.

Racism means treating someone as less because of their race.

Sometimes it is clear, like the killing of George Floyd by police.

Sometimes it is hidden in jokes or ideas about people based on looks.

Sexism means treating someone as less because of their gender.

The most common form is hatred of women.

Gender stereotypes are also sexism, assuming what someone can do based on their gender.

Materialism means caring too much about things – nice shoes, a big house – to show status.

This makes you selfish and leads to always comparing yourself to others.

And comparison steals happiness.

The race to get more can never be won.

How do you fight these “Four Horsemen”?

First, accept that everyone is different.

Putting people in boxes is wrong.

Second, use empathetic listening and try to understand instead of judging by tribe, race, gender, or things.

Finally, practice love.

When you really know someone, you see who they are beyond labels.

You find reasons to like them instead of seeing them as “others.”

The Four “-isms” hurt real leadership.

They feed the Red Wolf and stop belonging.

Fight them actively, not just quietly.

## RECLAIM YOUR ATTENTION

The most important freedom you have is choosing how to spend your time.

Ask someone in prison – they lost this freedom.

Ask someone with a drug problem – the drug controls them, not them.

Think about social media.

These platforms make billions by selling your attention to advertisers.

Their job is to keep you watching.

They are very good at it and get better every year.

Social media is like going to a mall with friends.

Even if you just want to hang out, you are tempted to buy things all the time.

The mall is built to get money from you.

Social media is built to take your time and attention.

Does this mean you must stop using social media?

Not always.

A doctor long ago said the poison is in the dose.

Even water can be harmful if you drink too much.

Social media works the same way.

Research shows both how long and what you see matters for your mind.

The key is to avoid quick pleasures that steal your time and keep you hooked.

Use social media with a plan for long-term good things.

Use it to build real friendships, find groups, or share good messages.

Ask yourself: How can I use social media in a good way?

Try this for one day and see what happens.

This is social media’s weakness – using your freedom to choose how you spend time.

Your attention is your most valuable thing.

Where you put it, your life follows.

Protect it well.

## GROWING FROM MISTAKES

Let’s finish with one of leadership’s best powers: learning from experience, even mistakes.

This means growth.

Start with a reality check.

You may watch Michael Jordan play and think, “I can play like that.”

Reality check – you are not Michael Jordan.

But you can be inspired to practice and improve.

Next, remember no one is born a leader.

Leadership is something you grow by living the ideas we talked about.

You also learn leadership from making mistakes.

You learn by doing things wrong and then doing them better.

When you make a mistake, you have a choice: ignore it or blame others, or admit it and learn.

Great leaders admit their mistakes.

The process is this:

First, see you made a mistake.

Second, fix it – say sorry, replace what you broke, make things better.

Third, learn the lesson.

What can you learn?

What will you do differently next time?

Fourth, promise to do better.

Finally, do this again and again to grow.

Remember, perfection is not your goal.

It is impossible and trying for it stops you.

Perfection can keep people from starting.

Growth is your goal.

Getting better is your goal.

Ask yourself: What would a perfect world look like?

The good part of learning from mistakes is you start making new kinds of mistakes.

You are always learning and changing.

Great leaders grow and get better by using mistakes as fuel.

You can do the same.

## FINAL SUMMARY

The main message of this Blink to Change Your Game by J.

Chad Mitchell is that you can be a leader, and the world needs you to lead.

Leadership is not about reaching a final place where you know everything.

It is about choosing every day to feed your Leadership Wolf.

To be kind and honest.

To seek to understand, not judge.

To lift others up.

To learn from mistakes.

To make small good choices that add up to big change.

The world needs leaders with good values and who care for others.

The world needs a change to stop greed, hate, and jealousy.

The world needs young leaders like you.
