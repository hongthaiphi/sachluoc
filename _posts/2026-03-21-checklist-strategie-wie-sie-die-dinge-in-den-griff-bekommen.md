### Synopsis

Atul Gawande is a surgeon. In his book *Checklist Strategy*, he shares surprising proof. This proof shows how a simple checklist can greatly reduce human mistakes. This is true in very complex jobs. Examples are flying planes, engineering, and medicine.

## Human knowledge is getting more complex. It's harder to keep track of.

Imagine you had a heart attack in 1950. Doctors knew very little about heart problems then. They would only give you pain medicine and tell you to rest. You could even smoke a cigarette while getting better. It would have been fine.

Luckily, our knowledge has grown a lot since then. Just 60 years ago, doctors did not know much about heart attacks. But today, we have many ways to treat them. These include blood pressure medicine, heart procedures, and even open-heart surgery. We also know many ways to prevent heart problems. For example, medicine to lower cholesterol and more exercise.

But sometimes, having a lot of knowledge in complex areas like medicine can be tricky. It can be both good and bad. The World Health Organization says we now know about 13,000 different health issues. These include syndromes, diseases, and injuries. Also, think about all the medicines and treatments we have. It's clear that no single doctor or even a whole team of doctors can know everything.  

This creates a problem: we are not perfect. We try to remember important information, organize it, and use it correctly all the time. Surgeons and nurses face huge pressure. They must know the newest medical tools. They must care for many patients at once. They also need to do many steps for complex medical tasks. At the same time, they must handle unexpected things that happen when treating sick people.

Clearly, we need a new tool. This tool will help us use our huge medical knowledge. It will also help us avoid deadly human mistakes.

## Blink 1 - Checklists help prevent serious mistakes.

A surgery team in San Francisco faced a very difficult situation. They thought they were treating a small cut. But after the surgeon made the first cut, a lot of blood came out of the patient. Then the team saw that the wound was 30 cm long.

It was a wound from a bayonet. The patient got it at a Halloween party. Sadly, no one on the medical team asked about the weapon used before the operation.

The man survived his injury. But problems like this, with missing or wrong information, happen often in medicine. It is quite scary. They can lead to serious problems or even death. In the USA, over 50 million surgeries are done each year. More than 150,000 patients die from these surgeries. A study says that about half of these problems and deaths could be stopped.

So, how can we make fewer mistakes?

Instead of spending millions on new medical tools, the answer is much simpler: use a checklist.

Checklists are simple lists. They show steps you need to do for a task. What is surprising is that easy steps, things everyone should know, are often forgotten. This can lead to very bad results. A checklist acts like a safety net. It makes sure we do not miss key things before we start. Once we check off the basic points, we feel more ready. Then we can deal with harder questions. These change from patient to patient.

If the team had used a checklist for the bayonet injury, they would have been ready for the bad, bloody surprise.

## Blink 2 - Checklists should be short. They should include all important steps and be easy to understand.

We often laugh at checklists. Many of us see them as just messy notes or fancy to-do lists. But if they cover all key points, are short, and are easy to use, checklists become very powerful tools.

The most important thing about a checklist is that it points out the „deadly parts“ of a task. These are steps that people often miss. But if you miss them, the results can be terrible. For example, it could be remembering to ask a patient about allergies before surgery.  

A good checklist must include these key points. But it must not become a long manual. Daniel Boorman, an expert pilot who makes checklists for Boeing, says 5 to 9 points are best. He also says there should be a time limit to go through a list. After about one minute, people stop paying attention. If it takes longer, life-saving steps can be missed.

A checklist should also be easy to use and clear. For example, it should be clear if you read the steps first and then do them (a „Read-Do“ list). Or if you do the steps first and then check them (a „Do-Confirm“ list). If not, people can misunderstand and use it wrongly.

Atul Gawande made an early version of his checklist. It confused one of the nurses just before surgery. It was not clear how to use the checklist. The nurse checked off all the steps before the surgery even started. Gawande had wanted her to read each step to the team and check it off during the surgery.

So, when we make a checklist, it must be clear. It must be easy to use. And it must only include the most important points.

## Blink 3 - Today's complex tasks cannot be done by one hero alone. We need teams.

In scary situations, we often wish for a hero. This hero would suddenly appear and fix all problems at once with their skills and experience. The idea that one person's knowledge is enough to solve our problems sounds good. But it is not real.

In complex and dangerous situations, the whole team matters.

Long ago, one builder would build and watch over a whole building project. Today, building even one project needs many experts to work together. These experts deal with things like machines, walls, waterproofing, and even pest control.  

Teamwork is very important, especially in emergencies and difficult times. In 2009, Captain Chesley B. Sullenberger landed a plane safely in the Hudson River. No one died. The media called him „Captain America“ and a hero. But Sullenberger said the safe landing was thanks to the whole team. Later, more details about the event came out. It was clear the plane could not have landed safely without Sullenberger, First Officer Jeffrey Skiles, and the rest of the crew working together.

In medicine, more than just one surgeon is needed for an operation. Anesthesiologists, nurses, and surgeons must all work together. They need to share their knowledge to make the surgery successful.  

When things are difficult or complex, we must agree that many people's actions and skills are needed. It's not just one person who makes a complex task succeed.

## Blink 4 - Good team communication is key in complex situations. Checklists can make it much better.

Imagine a flight crew landing a passenger plane. Now, think how bad it would be if the team could not talk to each other during this difficult task. The captain would not know if the sky was clear enough to land. The co-pilot would not know when or if to take control of the plane. The flight attendants would not know when to do safety checks.

Teams working on complex tasks need to communicate well. In the example above, the crew could follow a checklist. But without talking to each other, the checklist would be useless.

You can improve communication by adding steps to a checklist. These steps help information flow better. For example, Joe Salvia, a structural engineer from Boston, uses „submittal“ checklists. These lists tell construction team members when to update each other. They also show when to check each other's work before moving to the next building step.

Another thing you can add to a checklist to improve communication is a „huddle“. This lets team members introduce themselves at the start of a task. They can also talk about any problems that might come up. Gawande made this a must-do part of his „Safe Surgery Saves Lives“ checklist. He did this after a study showed that people work worse together if they do not know each other's names. And it worked: after they started using the „huddle,“ team communication got much better. The number of medical problems that could have been avoided dropped quickly.

Good team communication is key when under stress. Teams tend to follow checklists better if they have set times to talk.

## Blink 5 - Medical checklists have already saved many lives.

It is hard to believe that a simple checklist can have a big effect on the world. But medical programs that study checklists have shown this. They show that checklists prevent mistakes, save money, and most importantly, save lives.

Peter Pronovost is an expert in critical care. He started a study called the „Keystone Initiative“. Its goal was to lower infections from central line catheters. Patients in intensive care often get these catheters. They often cause infections, for example, if touched by unclean hands. These infections can lead to deadly problems for patients. Pronovost used a checklist to see if it could reduce infections. It worked very well: In 18 months, the program saved 175 million dollars and 1,500 lives.

Gawande and the World Health Organization got ideas from Pronovost, engineers, and aviators. They made a checklist for the „Safe Surgery Saves Lives“ program. It was tested in eight hospitals around the world. Hospitals were asked to use this surgery checklist. It had 19 key points. These ranged from talking about how much blood a patient might lose to making sure it was the right patient. The results were amazing: deaths from surgery dropped by 47% in all eight hospitals.

The author saw for himself how powerful checklists are. During a surgery check for expected blood loss, he found that no extra blood units were ready for the patient. Gawande did not think the patient would need it. But to follow the checklist, he asked for the blood to be ready. During the surgery, Gawande accidentally made a cut that caused the patient's heart to stop. The extra blood saved the patient's life. Gawande is sure that without the checklist, he would have killed the patient.

A simple checklist is not just a powerful tool. It can also decide between life and death.

## Blink 6 - Checklists can be used everywhere. They work well in many places.

Many of us work under great stress in complex jobs. These include finance or the restaurant business. Like medicine, these jobs need carefulness and exactness. In these stressful areas, checklists can be very useful. They can help us work better.

Jody Adams from Rialto Restaurant in Boston gives a good example. Most people would call Jody's checklists „recipes.“ But recipes and checklists do the same thing: they tell us what to do and when. The restaurant staff has all recipes in the kitchen. They also use checklists to make sure they follow each guest's special requests.

Once the dishes are ready to serve, there is a final check. The head chef or Jody herself does this check. They make sure Jody's standards are met. The checklist system helps Rialto serve fine dishes to its customers every time. They make sure to meet customer wishes. So, it is not surprising that Jody has won many awards for her skill. Her restaurant is often on „best of“ lists.

Bankers can use checklists to avoid needless risks. These risks come from quick and poorly researched decisions. For example, „Cook,“ an unknown investor who runs a multi-billion dollar fund, uses a „Day Three Checklist.“ This helps him and his team decide if they should invest in a company. The checklist gives Cook a big advantage: it makes him efficient. This careful and quick way to check investments gives him a clear edge over other investors.

Whether it's saving lives, giving great restaurant experiences, or making a lot of money: The simple checklist is a tool that can be used for many things. It gives great results and can be used in many different situations.

## Summary

Checklists are more than just to-do lists for lazy people. They can be very good tools for workers and experts. Using well-made checklists in complex situations helps us avoid deadly mistakes or dangerous oversights.

What you can do:

**Use a checklist. **We can be very stubborn about not making checklists for ourselves. This is especially true when we think we know exactly what we are doing. But a checklist helps us a lot, especially in tough jobs with a lot of pressure. One reason is that we often skip or forget „simple“ tasks. We wrongly think it's more important to focus on the harder parts. But these simple things are often very important. A checklist makes sure we don't miss necessary steps. Try it, and you will see that you make fewer mistakes with a checklist.

**Need help with team projects? Try a „huddle.“ **At the start of a team project, make sure everyone knows each other's name and role. This helps communication and sharing of information. Take time for a team huddle at the start of every project. Let team members introduce themselves and their roles. Let key members share what they expect and talk about problems that might happen during the project.
