**Author:** Thich Nhat Hanh | **Category:** Consciencia y felicidad## ¿QUÉ GANO YO CON ESTO? APRENDE A TRANSFORMAR TU SUFRIMIENTO EN ALEGRÍA PROFUNDA.

Suffering is a part of life.

Our instinct tells us to turn away and try to avoid pain.

But this does not work.

In fact, it makes the pain worse.

However, there is a way of living that can free you from suffering and bring joy to every moment you live.

If you follow it, you will live deeply and in harmony with the world.

Your relationships will get better, and peace will fill you.

This way of living is based on the Four Noble Truths of the Buddha, which are teachings anyone can follow.

The end of suffering and true happiness are within your reach; you just need to learn how to practice the Buddha’s teachings.

Do you want to learn how to use the Buddha’s teachings to live with more peace and fullness?

Then, let’s begin!

## LAS ENSEÑANZAS DEL BUDA PROPORCIONAN UN CAMINO PARA TRANSFORMAR EL SUFRIMIENTO EN ALEGRÍA.

When he was 29 years old, Siddhartha Gautama left his home to find a way to end human suffering.

After six years of meditation, he sat under a bodhi tree and decided not to move until he reached enlightenment.

The next morning, he had a great insight and became the Buddha, full of love and understanding.

This awakening flowed like water.

The Buddha needed to pour that water into different containers so his followers could understand its different parts.

These containers are called the Four Noble Truths and the Noble Eightfold Path.

When you learn and practice these teachings, you can find peace and happiness even in hard times.

The Four Noble Truths work like a cycle that helps you move toward peace.

The First Noble Truth is dukkha, or suffering.

Since you are alive, you will experience suffering in your body or mind at some point.

The Buddha was also human.

He too experienced suffering.

This shared experience connects you with the Buddha.

You can bring your pain to him, and he will welcome you with compassion.

Samudaya, meaning the origin of your suffering, is the Second Noble Truth.

In this cycle, you connect with your suffering to explore it.

By doing this with love and kindness, you can find the cause of your pain.

After you find the source of your suffering, you can practice the Third Noble Truth: nirodha, or stopping the creation of suffering.

Here, you stop doing things that cause you pain.

This makes healing possible.

The first three truths lead to the Fourth Noble Truth: marga, or the path.

This path is called the Noble Eightfold Path.

By following its eight practices, you can avoid the causes of suffering and live deeply and peacefully instead.

Each of the Four Noble Truths has three stages called turns.

These are recognition, encouragement, and realization.

In the next sections, we will look at each stage of the Truths.

As you learn the Buddha’s teachings, remember they are for practice, not just ideas.

When you commit to practicing, you can remove the blocks in your life that stop you from feeling joy.

According to the Buddha, denying our suffering is like carrying a very heavy load.

## EL PRIMER PASO PARA SANAR ES ACEPTAR TU SUFRIMIENTO.

Suffering shows itself in many ways, from eating unhealthy food to doing jobs that hurt others.

Because it is painful, most people try to run away from it.

But really, this is not possible: our pain is part of us.

Healing cannot happen unless you choose to accept your pain.

Instead of seeing it as something to run from, see it as a Sacred Truth, a way to grow and be free.

Hold your suffering gently, like a mother who carefully lifts a crying baby.

Only then can you start to understand the nature of your pain.

Imagine your whole body hurts, but you don’t know why.

You go to the doctor, and they check you to find the cause.

Like a diagnosis needs many steps, the First Noble Truth has many turns.

The first turn of the First Noble Truth is like being checked by a doctor.

Just as a doctor sees your pain, you need to see you feel pain to study its nature.

The pain can be physical, related to your body, or psychological.

In any case, accept it fully, even if it is hard feelings like hate or distress.

You can do this by lovingly telling your suffering that you see it and you will care for it.

When you stop running from your pain, the second turn can start: encouragement.

This phase gives you a chance to look deeply at your suffering and understand it better.

It is like when a doctor does tests to find exactly what is wrong.

But instead of blood tests or x-rays, you use meditation to know your suffering well.

In the final turn of the First Noble Truth, realization, you get the results of your study.

Like a doctor’s diagnosis, these results let you name your suffering and describe it clearly.

The result of these three turns is that you stop avoiding your pain and you can name it truly.

This naming will bring some relief.

Though your suffering is not over yet, you will be ready to move to the next step of healing.

## EL SEGUNDO PASO PARA SANAR ES IDENTIFICAR QUÉ ES LO QUE ALIMENTA TU SUFRIMIENTO.

In his teachings, the Buddha told a story of a young couple crossing a desert with their two-year-old child.

Long before the trip ended, they ran out of food.

The couple knew the only way to survive was to kill their child and eat his meat to last the journey.

And that is what they did.

But every time they ate the child’s body, their suffering grew, and they cried.

Though this example is extreme, many of us are like that couple.

We keep feeding our suffering.

We do this by taking in toxins.

These toxins are not always easy to see, so we must watch all we take in and check if it causes us pain.

Knowing what you consume is the first turn of the Second Noble Truth, which, as you remember, is about where suffering begins.

The things you feed yourself come in many forms.

The most obvious are food and drinks.

They can keep you healthy and able to help others, or they can cause physical or mental pain.

You also feed your senses and mind with movies you watch, music you listen to, books you read, and talks you have.

If you feel tired after these, the things you consumed are probably toxic.

You can protect yourself by choosing carefully what to take part in.

Your intentions and goals are also kinds of food.

Ask yourself what drives your actions.

Are you trying to get more money, things, or fame, or are you working to help others?

Lastly, pay attention to your consciousness, shaped by your past, your family’s past, and society’s past.

You can choose to feed your consciousness with love and kindness, or with hate and ignorance.

Once you know what toxins are in your diet, you can enter the encouragement turn by setting the goal to change your habits.

In this stage, you see your happiness will grow if you stop taking in toxins.

The final turn, realization, comes when you put your goal into action.

Using mindfulness, watch what you consume and avoid toxins.

This way, you care for your body and mind and reduce suffering.

## EL TERCER PASO PARA LA SANACIÓN ES RECONOCER LA ALEGRÍA DEL BIENESTAR.

Think about when you were a child.

Hopefully, you enjoyed the gift of a strong and healthy body.

But you probably did not appreciate your health until you had an accident or got sick.

You may have only noticed how wonderful health is once you lost it.

The Third Noble Truth helps you reach well-being by valuing what you have.

Most people forget to be grateful for not being in pain until pain appears.

But by practicing mindfulness, you can feel thankful for your well-being, and this will make your happiness grow.

Before the first turn of the Third Noble Truth, you are like a child who happily takes their health for granted.

But when you see that you feel peace when you are free from suffering, you start the next cycle.

Even if you feel pain, you can reach this turn by remembering moments when you felt joy or saw joy in others.

By doing this, you see well-being is possible.

Encouragement starts when you actively look for joy.

You can imagine joy as much as you like, but you will not feel it unless you create it.

Being alive is the biggest miracle of all.

That is why joy can be found in the smallest acts, even washing the dishes!

By seeing that your suffering is not worth it, you can connect with deep and strong joy.

Like suffering, joy does not last forever, so learn to grow it so you always have it nearby.

Use mindfulness to see all the wonders of life.

They are a constant source of happiness.

When you can grow joy, you will realize it is not a weak or rare feeling as you thought.

Since your joy now comes from the wonder of being alive, it is strong and true.

This turn will get you ready for the Fourth Noble Truth: the path out of suffering.

When you begin this stage, you know the path exists but don’t know how to walk it.

You must encourage yourself to learn, think, and then act from what you learned.

By doing this, you move away from pain, step by step.

When you decide to stop feeding suffering, a path will open that leads to well-being.

We will explore how to walk it in the next sections.

## PARA RECORRER EL CAMINO DE LA FELICIDAD, DEBES DESAFIAR TUS PERCEPCIONES.

In 1966, Thich Nhat Hanh went to a peace protest in Philadelphia.

A reporter asked if he was from North Vietnam, which supported communism, or South Vietnam, which was pro-American.

Nhat Hanh said he was from the middle.

He wanted the reporter to see beyond labels we put on others and to face the real moment.

To follow the Noble Eightfold Path, you must start by practicing Right View.

In this practice, you see that change is possible and comes from understanding suffering.

But Right View does not stop there.

It asks you to keep checking your views because how you see things affects what you believe is true.

Imagine ten people looking at a cloud.

Each one will see something different: a coat, a dog, a hammer.

They will interpret the cloud in their own way, based on their experiences.

Like cloud watchers, your views are shaped by your feelings.

For example, if you had a painful relationship with your father, you might act angry toward someone who just reminds you of him.

This way, your views can be wrong and cause pain to you and others.

To reach Right View, you need to question what shaped your views.

When you free yourself from them, you get a clear view that brings understanding.

Right View is seeing the true reality.

The second practice of the Noble Eightfold Path, Right Thought, will come when Right View is strong.

Your thoughts are how your mind talks.

With Right Thought, this talk becomes clear and helpful.

Practicing Right Thought means seeing things as they really are, which is hard.

Often, our thoughts are about past sadness or future worries.

But these thoughts are usually not based on deep understanding, so they are not useful.

Like questioning your views, you can question your thoughts by asking if you are sure they are right.

It also helps to get used to asking what you are doing right now.

This brings your attention to the present so you can fully focus on what you are doing without useless thoughts.

## PARA SANAR, DEBES PRESTAR ATENCIÓN.

Imagine you are a smoker.

You probably already know that smoking is bad for your health.

But mindfulness can help you understand the effects of smoking beyond yourself.

By thinking deeply about your habit, you will see how it affects your family and the people around you.

It even affects the whole community.

Knowing this can be enough to help you quit smoking.

This way, mindfulness allows change.

Right mindfulness is at the heart of the Buddha’s teachings, connecting all parts of the Noble Eightfold Path.

Practicing it means finding loving chances each day to focus on the present moment.

This makes you fully present.

And being fully present brings healing joy.

Right mindfulness helps you handle all your feelings, good, bad, or neutral.

By welcoming and watching your feelings with mindfulness, you become calmer.

Instead of being overwhelmed, your feelings become more familiar as you pay attention, making them less strong.

Right mindfulness also helps others.

Paying attention to the people in your life is the best way to show them love.

You can do this by simply looking them in the eye, showing you know they are with you.

By paying attention, you see when they are suffering and can notice their pain.

Often, this is enough to give them some relief.

When you practice Right Mindfulness, you create the Buddha’s energy inside you and in the world you live in.

This energy helps you see and listen deeply so you can talk in ways that ease suffering.

Right mindfulness leads to the next practice of the Noble Eightfold Path: Right Speech.

When you pay attention to your words, you can check if they help or hurt others.

Right speech is based on deep listening, which comes from mindfulness.

To speak correctly, you must listen and respond to others instead of talking only about yourself.

Practicing Right Speech means listening with kindness, without judging or giving advice.

By listening deeply, you can ease others’ suffering and grow your connection with them.

Both sides suffer when communication breaks, so fixing it should be a priority.

Sometimes, just ten minutes of deep listening and kind words can heal a wound.

## PARA VIVIR EN PAZ, NO HAGAS DAÑO.

One time, a woman asked Thich Nhat Hanh why she should stop drinking alcohol.

She had been drinking two glasses of wine a week for 20 years without harming herself. Did she really have to quit?

Nhat Hanh said that even if she was not at risk of alcoholism, she could not know if her children were.

By quitting alcohol, she would be helping her children and, by extension, society.

Right Action, the next practice of the Noble Eightfold Path, is based on respecting all life.

This includes you, other people, plants, animals, and even minerals.

This respect stops you from harming or killing, or supporting those who kill.

Every time you hurt someone or the world, you hurt yourself.

So, to stop this cycle of harm, you must practice non-violence.

Right Action asks you to look beyond your own interests and grow kindness toward others.

This means stopping exploitation, social unfairness, and profits made by hurting others.

It is important to try to live a life that helps the well-being of all.

Sexual responsibility is also part of Right Action.

This teaching says that loneliness cannot be healed by sex without kindness, understanding, or good communication.

Without these, sex can hurt you and others, so be aware of who you are with and why.

Right Action leads to another practice of the Noble Eightfold Path: Right Livelihood.

Work that is not based on love and kindness will cause suffering to you and others.

If your job does not help life or the planet, at least it should not cause harm.

Use mindfulness at work to behave in a way that creates a peaceful and happy place.

This means being aware of how you move between meetings, answer the phone, and interact with coworkers and clients.

Remember that how you choose to live affects others’ jobs.

For example, if no one ate meat, there would be no butchers, whose job involves killing animals.

Meat eaters share responsibility for that killing with butchers, no matter how humanely the animals are raised and slaughtered.

Societies should work to create jobs that do not cause harm so everyone can practice Right Livelihood.

## LA ALEGRÍA NACE DE LA TRANQUILIDAD.

Once there was a monk in China’s Tang dynasty who meditated carefully day and night.

He was proud that he practiced more than other monks, thinking that sitting all day would make him a Buddha.

But no matter how hard he tried, he did not find the enlightenment he looked for.

Right Effort is the energy that helps us practice the Noble Eightfold Path.

Like the Chinese monk, you might think you are working hard in your practice, but if your effort is not based on Right View, then your effort is not right.

So, how do you know if your effort is right?

There will be a feeling of ease in what you do, and it will bring you joy.

Right Effort tends to the healthy parts of your nature and keeps the unhealthy parts where they cannot grow.

The healthy parts include love, happiness, loyalty, and making peace.

Cultivating them every day will bring you joy, which will make these qualities grow.

Joy is at the heart of Right Effort.

When you practice mindfulness, you will feel joy and interest in what you do.

This creates a feeling of calm.

It may take time to reach this calm feeling, but the more you practice, the faster you will reach it.

Right Concentration, the last practice of the Noble Eightfold Path, supports Right Effort.

It is the practice of focusing your mind on one point.

Concentration helps you connect deeply with the present moment.

In it, you are steady and calm.

When you live deeply in the moment, concentration comes naturally.

And in this state, insight will arise.

Right Concentration is a door to see how deeply all things are connected.

For example, if you focus on a piece of fruit,

you will start to understand that the fruit is also the flower it came from, the earth that fed it, and the rain that watered it.

You will begin to see that you are not separate but are made from your family and ancestors going back in time.

This will help you see that all life is as valuable as yours, inspiring you to cherish life in all its wonderful forms.

In this Blink to The Heart of the Buddha’s Teachings, you have learned that suffering is a natural part of life.

## RESUMEN FINAL

You will surely experience and cause it.

But this does not mean you cannot feel deep joy and happiness every day.

If you want to live a way that frees you from suffering, first you must accept your pain and study it with mindfulness.

By doing this, you can find out what caused your pain and what keeps feeding it.

With this knowledge, you will be able to change your actions, views, and thoughts on purpose to end your suffering and the suffering of those around you.
