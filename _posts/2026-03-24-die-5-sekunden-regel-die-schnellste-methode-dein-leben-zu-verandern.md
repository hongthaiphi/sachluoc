### Synopsis

We all put things off. We also avoid and delay tasks we should do. But we can take back control of our actions. TV host Mel Robbins did this with her *5-Second Rule* (2017). Every time she almost gives up, she counts down from five. Then she simply does what she really needs to do. This fun summary will show you how this works. It will also show if it can help you too.

## What's in it for you: An amazingly simple trick to do what's really important.

Imagine you know what you should do: work, file your taxes, call an office. Speak up about a problem or finally share your opinion. Fold laundry, make food, or just get up. But something pulls you in another direction. Suddenly, everything else seems more appealing than your tasks. So you *procrastinate*. You delay, avoid, and postpone. You know this will cause problems later. You will also feel bad about it. Many people might know this feeling.

Now, imagine you had a very simple but effective trick. It stops you from putting things off. You stop your thoughts as they start to wander. You count backward from five. You close your eyes: Five, four, three, two, one… Then you do what is actually planned. You type the next letters in the document on the screen. You are brave and speak up. You get out of bed and face the day. Sounds too good to be true? Let's see after this summary, okay?

## Blink 1 - A Simple Tool for Better Decisions

It was six in the morning on a dark winter day in Boston. Mel Robbins woke up to her loud alarm clock. There were times when she got out of bed with energy and in a good mood. But her life had changed. She had no job, money worries, and a drinking problem. She was used to delaying the start of her day. She would use the snooze button as much as possible.

But for some reason, she did something completely different that morning. Instead of silencing her alarm as usual, she started to count backward. She was still in bed with her eyes closed. But she followed a sudden urge to start a quiet countdown: "Five, four, three, two, one." And she got up. At that moment, she knew something in her life had changed. She had just found a secret weapon.

She started to use the five-second countdown trick in other situations. For example, to get herself to go jogging on days she didn't want to. Or to finally write her resume. Every time she caught herself putting things off, she counted down from five. And each time, she beat her inner resistance.

The idea is simple. The short countdown stops your subconscious mind. It stops it before it can make its usual excuses. This brings your focus back to what you actually want to do. With regular practice, you learn to break bad patterns. You also build better habits. This is especially helpful for people who have been waiting for years. They wait for things in their lives to get better on their own.

People often say you should wait patiently in life. You should wait until the right chances appear. But Mel Robbins thinks this is nonsense. She thinks it's smarter to take action yourself. You should actively create opportunities. She calls this the "Power of the Push". And the Five-Second Rule can be that small push you need.

## Blink 2 - Use Your Short Window for Brave First Steps

On a cold December evening in 1955, 42-year-old African American woman Rosa Parks sat on a bus in Alabama. She refused to give her seat to a white man. This small act of resistance was a key spark. It started the historic Civil Rights Movement in the USA. This example shows that you don't always need big actions. Small actions can also start important changes.

The Five-Second Rule will not improve civil rights. But it works on the same idea. Counting down from five is not a huge life decision. But it can start a series of changes. These changes can make you a braver person.

Rosa Parks was arrested for disturbing public order. Four days after her arrest, protests began. People protested against the racist seating on the city buses. Activists called for a bus boycott. They asked a 26-year-old Baptist preacher to become the voice of their movement. This unknown preacher later wrote that everything happened so fast. He had no time to think about his decision. He also wrote that he probably would have said no otherwise. His name was Martin Luther King Jr.

Neither Parks nor King had seen themselves as very brave people before this. They certainly didn't see themselves as heroes. Neither of them had planned to stand up against unfairness in society. But both reached a point where their instincts and core values came together. At that moment, they felt the Power of the Push. It moved them past their old limits.

We all have voices inside us that demand safety. These voices sometimes take away our courage. But the Five-Second Rule can open a small window of time. In this time, you can still act. You can move towards new chances. Every day offers chances to break out of your usual routine. You always have five seconds to step out of your comfort zone.

You don't have to be extremely brave to make a change. Rosa Parks was shy and quiet. Martin Luther King struggled with self-doubt his whole life. But both managed to overcome their fears. And you can do it too.

## Blink 3 - Every Moment Is the Right Time

Sometimes it takes very little: a raised hand in a meeting. It can help you finally share your opinion. It can change how your teammates see you. Or a sincere compliment. It can make someone smile. But even small actions like these need a decision first. And that decision can be put off with an excuse. You might say "now is not the right time". Before we know it, this "not now" becomes a theme throughout our lives.

The Five-Second Rule can be the small push you need. It can help you break this cycle. You can finally make the decisions you've been putting off for so long.

Nobody wants to be remembered as someone who did nothing or said nothing. Yet most of us get stuck in these situations. A study on feedback at work found that 85% of employees held back criticism. They did not share it with their managers. They were all waiting for the famous "right moment". But this perfect timing is a myth. If we wait too long for it, life just passes us by.

Even very talented people sometimes need someone. They need someone to free them from their  inner hesitation. For example, Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak hesitated for a long time in 1977. This was before he quit his safe office job. He was very scared of the risk. His friends pushed him to finally take the leap. The rest is history. His success was less about timing. It was more about the courage to not put off that key step any longer.

Another example is the author E. L. James. She did not wait for a big publishing deal or perfect conditions. She was a working mother. She wrote a novel in her limited free time. She later published it herself. The title was *Fifty Shades of Grey*. The book sold over one million copies in just four days. None of this would have happened if James had kept waiting for the right moment.

So: Stop waiting. Start doing. You have five seconds to make the needed decision.

## Blink 4 - Get Moving Before Your Feelings Scare You

Many people like to be inspired by professional athletes. This is not surprising. They show us what our bodies can do. They push past their limits with training and discipline. Their achievements sometimes seem almost superhuman. Yet, they have a surprisingly simple secret. Top athletes also get tired, exhausted, and frustrated. But they have learned to separate themselves from these feelings. They do this for a short and important moment. And in that moment, they find the strength to keep going.

They know that physical feelings are suggestions, not commands. And you can learn to ignore these suggestions. You do this when you have a big goal in mind.

This is interesting because most people make emotional decisions. They do not make rational ones. According to neuroscientist Antonio Damasio, 95% of our decisions are guided by feelings. In other words: We don't think before we act. We *feel* before we act.

For his studies, Damasio looked at people with brain damage. This damage affected how they processed emotions. He gave his subjects who lacked emotions decisions to make. They could analyze their choices logically. They could weigh the pros, cons, and possible results. But they could not make even one decision. From this, he concluded: Humans are not "thinking machines that can also feel". Instead, we are "feeling machines that can also think".

You can use this knowledge to use the Five-Second Rule. Stop letting fear, doubts, or laziness stop you from your goals. Use the five seconds to overcome these emotions. Do what you have planned. In psychology, this is called "cognitive intervention". The idea behind it comes from Aristotle's teaching on virtues: "Do good, be good." In other words: You must act differently than before. You do this if you want to feel differently. And this is exactly where the Five-Second Rule helps you. It gives you the short time you need. You can use courage and logic to overcome your inner resistance and become active.

## Blink 5 - The Best Way to Get Work Done Is Just to Start

Modern technology often has a strange effect. Officially, all smart devices and apps should make us more productive. But in the end, we sit on the sofa scrolling and swiping. We get lost in some "rabbit hole". We just procrastinate faster and more effectively than before.

There is a difference between harmless and harmful procrastination. Taking a quick look at TikTok before checking your emails is quite harmless. It becomes harmful when you put off important things. You know that delaying them will cause serious problems.

For a long time, experts thought that procrastination was simply due to bad time management. People who put off their duties were seen as lacking willpower or self-discipline. But today we know that procrastination is not the same as laziness. It is always also a way to deal with stress. Psychologist Timothy Pychyl from Carleton University in Ottawa explains it like this: We all want instant rewards subconsciously. And putting things off gives us just that: a short feeling of happiness. It simply distracts us from our daily stress for a moment. 

The Five-Second Rule helps you break out of this pattern. It replaces your old habit of escaping. It replaces it with a healthier habit. Pychyl says: "The best way to get a task done is to just start." And this is exactly where the short countdown comes in. As soon as you notice you want to avoid something – and that you're drawn to the fridge or Instagram – you count down: five, four, three, two, one… and start. 

With each successful countdown, you strengthen your self-efficacy. This is the feeling of having things in your own hands. Procrastination is also just a form of losing control. And with the Five-Second Countdown, you can take back control of your behavior.

## Blink 6 - Don't Waste Time on Unnecessary Worries

It's no surprise that we spend so much time worrying and overthinking. After all, from a young age, we are always told: "Watch out!", "Be careful!", "Wear a jacket, or you'll get sick!" This is a main reason why, as adults, we constantly worry about things. These things are outside our control. 

It is very likely that we will later deeply regret one day. We will regret wasting so much precious life on needless worries. Sociologist Karl Pillemer from Cornell University in America found this out. For a long-term study, he asked over 1,200 seniors. He asked them what really matters in life, in their opinion. One of the most common answers was that people regretted wasting so much time on pointless worries.

Life is too precious to constantly be afraid or stressed. So, don't let yourself feel regret. Use the Five-Second Rule to get back control of your thoughts. Enjoy your life to the fullest. Get used to paying attention to your mood. As soon as you notice your thoughts running wild and worrying, take a break. Take a deep breath. Take five full seconds to bring your attention back to the present moment.

When you reach "one", ask yourself these two questions: "What am I thankful for right now? And what do I want to remember later?" Both questions guide your focus to things that are truly important to you. These include your relationships and special projects. No matter how bad your day is: There are always more reasons to be thankful. There are more reasons than to drive yourself crazy. So, every now and then, take five seconds. Zoom out and see the big picture.

## Conclusion

Nothing in life is set in stone. Not your habits, nor your attitude. Even your character traits can change. So stop leaving your decisions to chance. Stop letting your impulses or outdated routines decide for you. Whenever your thoughts wander, or you avoid what's important, remember the 5-Second Rule. Count backward from five. Bring your focus back to what matters. Get up. Start working. Say what you should have said a long time ago. And be thankful for what you have.
