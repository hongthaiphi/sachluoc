Reading time: 18 minutes

### Synopsis

*The Startup Strategy* is a guide. It shows how we can use methods from startup companies. We can use them for our own career success. This helps us become more adaptable, build relationships, and take career opportunities.

In a world where global competition harms whole industries, old career paths often lead nowhere. So, everyone needs to work like a business owner. This way, they can stay successful for a long time.

## Find your way into the startup mindset.

In the past, a skilled worker's career path was often clear. You started at the lowest level in a big company. Then, you slowly moved up, like on an escalator. Over time, you got higher positions and better pay.

This 'escalator' has now stopped. Big economic forces like globalization and technology caused this. It has stopped at every level. Fewer people are retiring. There are few entry-level jobs. Middle managers often wait for promotions that do not come. If you have relied on this escalator for your career, you should be worried now.

But luckily, you can still succeed without the escalator. Startup companies always live with uncertainty and change. To do well in your career today, you need to use a startup mindset. The most important lesson is: Be flexible and alert. Always see yourself as a 'permanent beta' version. This means you are a work in progress, open to learning and growing.

Always make learning important! Often, 20 years of experience is just one year, repeated 20 times. In 'permanent beta' mode, every year should be full of new challenges. A comfortable job might seem low-risk. But it actually puts you in more danger in the long run. If you are used to adapting all the time, no big change can harm you. You will always be able to recover. But if you are used to doing nothing on the career escalator, an unexpected event like losing your job could end in disaster.

## Blink 1 - Know your competitive advantage.

There is always competition for everything good in this world. This includes customers, jobs, or good partners. A startup company needs to get an advantage over its competitors. You also need to find your competitive advantage.

A strong competitive advantage comes from three things working together. The first is your *strengths*. These include 'hard assets' like the money you have. They also include 'soft assets' like your skills, experience, and education. Over time, these strengths change their value. This is because we must always compare them to the competition. So, you should choose areas and markets where your strengths are clearly better than your competitors'.

The second factor comes from inside you: your goals and values. These show what is important to you and what you want for your future. This is very important for your success. Someone who loves their work and does it with passion will always work faster and better. This is true compared to someone who does not feel that passion.

The last factor is market realities. These are the needs of the world around you. Your competitive advantages are useless if nobody cares about them. But the right advantages can help you. You can gain from market trends and new technology, instead of being harmed by them.

These three factors change all the time for each person. Also, it is impossible to know them for sure. For example, few of us truly know what we are passionate about. The best way is to make guesses about these three parts. Then, adjust them as you move forward. 

## Blink 2 - Be flexible and well-prepared with Plan A, B, and Z.

Before Flickr became a very successful website for uploading photos, it was an online game. Sharing photos was just one of its popular features by chance. Before Sheryl Sandberg became COO of Facebook, she worked for the World Bank, the US Treasury, and even Google. At first, the paths of Sandberg and Flickr seem random and not connected. But this kind of changing path is common for many smart people and successful companies.

Of course, it is important to make plans that fit your competitive advantages. But the world around you changes. Your understanding of your interests and passions also changes. So, you must be able to adapt your plans. You can do this best with A-B-Z planning.

Plan A is what you are doing right now. It is what you currently see as your competitive advantage. As you grow, you might make small changes. But usually, you stay in the same area. A good Plan A is flexible and leaves room for many possible Plan B options.

Plan B is what you think about when Plan A starts to look less appealing. This alternative plan is often connected to Plan A. It allows you to stay partly in that field. When you shift your focus, Plan B becomes your new Plan A. Then you must define a new Plan B.

Finally, Plan Z is your safety net. It is like your parents' home, which can always offer a place to stay in an emergency. This plan is something that keeps you going if all else fails. Plan Z gives you the confidence to switch to Plan B. You know that even in the worst situation, you will not end up with nothing.

## Blink 3 - Show creativity and persistence when dealing with opportunities.

Sometimes, life offers you a chance for a big career step. This is a special opportunity to move up quickly. If you take this chance, it promises great success.

For example, George Clooney was an actor. For twelve years, he only got small roles in less important TV shows. Then he heard about a new hospital show called *Emergency Room*. He saw it as his big chance. He convinced the producer to let him audition. The show became a huge success, and the rest is history.

But these chances do not come gift-wrapped or with a big sign. They are very rare and hard to find. You cannot plan them in your diary. Too often, they are missed because we want to keep all our options open. But if you do not take the chance, it is lost.

To move forward in your career, you must chase these chances. Do it with a clear goal, with creativity, and with persistence.

The lodging website Airbnb also showed us how. The company believed in its idea. But it had trouble paying its daily costs. Instead of giving up, they became creative. They sold special election-themed cereals – *Obama O’s* and *Cap’n McCains*. This helped them fund their main business before it made a profit.

For persistence, we can learn from the music startup Pandora. It faced problems like evictions and legal cases for almost ten years. Then it finally made a big breakthrough.

## Blink 4 - Use your network to create more lucky chances in your life.

The most interesting jobs are rarely found in public advertisements. We also cannot buy career breakthroughs. Finding a chance for success often depends on being in the right place at the right time. Luckily, you can make this more likely.

First, no good luck will find you while you are in bed. Instead, you must actively look for lucky chances. Gather people around you who can help you. As one entrepreneur once said: "The best way to create lucky breaks is to start many things."

Joining or starting small, informal groups is a great way to meet new interesting people. These could be book clubs, beekeeping groups, or conferences. The best groups to help you are those with skilled people who live close by. They should also have common interests and experiences. In such groups, people like to share. There is a strong spirit of working together. They cooperate even with little competition.

A great example of such a network is the 'PayPal Mafia'. This group is made up of former PayPal leaders. Even though it is an informal network, it gives its members many chances. They can share ideas and work on projects together. This is true even if they sometimes compete. Some members have helped create some of Silicon Valley's biggest success stories. They did this as founders or investors. Examples include LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube, and Yelp.

## Blink 5 - Take smart risks where others hesitate.

Risks are a normal part of life. Almost every opportunity worth taking has some risks.

Different people think about risks in different ways. These ideas also change over time. Usually, younger people are more willing to take risks.

Generally, people overestimate the dangers that come with an opportunity. This is due to 'negativity bias'. We often think more about possible problems than possible benefits.

Many people miss big chances because they try to avoid risks. This is ironic. When competing for career chances, you can gain an advantage. Take smart risks. Actively look for opportunities that others underestimate because they see them as risky.

When judging risks, the main question should be: Could you live with the worst possible outcome? If a risk could make you lose all your savings and get into a lot of debt, you should not take it. Instead, always remember that you can turn back and use Plan B.

## Blink 6 - Focus on both quality and quantity in your relationships.

A startup company needs a team to survive. You also need to build a professional network around you. This network can help your career opportunities grow very fast.

Sadly, networking can easily lead to constantly chasing other people. This can seem like you are just using people, and not real. Instead, you should build true relationships. Focus on quality, not quantity. For this, it is important to know that a good relationship is not just about gaining something. It is about helping each other.

A good way to start is by helping the other person. A small, non-material gesture, like useful advice or a helpful introduction to someone else, is a perfect way to begin a new relationship. Usually, the heart of your network should be professional allies. These are people you trust. You can ask them for advice often. You work with them on career opportunities. These deep connections often need both time and being physically close.

Outside this core, you have your loose connections and acquaintances. These are people you know from school or met at a conference. But you do not spend much time with them. A wide network means you have connections in places and industries beyond your usual starting point. Studies show that new career opportunities often come from these loose connections, even if they seem 'surface-level'.

The strongest professional networks have both circles. They have a few deep, professional relationships. And they have a wide network of loose contacts.

## Blink 7 - Use the combined knowledge of your network.

Few people use the full potential of their professional network. But a strong network definitely gives you access to interesting people. It also gives you news about new career chances. And it lets you gain knowledge from many different experts.

Imagine you are thinking about a career change. You want to try working in the technology industry. To understand this career option better, you decide to use 'network intelligence'. This means using the experiences and knowledge within your network.

First, you use LinkedIn to search your extended network. These are people you mostly know through two other people. This network is much bigger than you think; it likely includes millions of people! You can certainly ask to be introduced to these people. This is because both connectors know either you or the person you want to meet.

After some searching, you find someone. This person works for a big tech company. You ask to be introduced to them. A few weeks later, you meet for lunch. She tells you about career options in her industry. She even mentions that her company has a job opening. You ask yourself if this is the chance you are waiting for. You decide to ask your network for advice.

You get opinions from people who know this industry well. You also get advice from personal friends. They can tell you if this career option fits your priorities and personality.

Lastly, you ask the top third of your network – friends, colleagues, and acquaintances – about changing careers. You hear some encouraging stories. After carefully thinking about all the information, you can make a confident and well-informed decision.

## Summary

The main message of this book is:

**We can learn a lot from a startup company for our own careers. To succeed in today's busy job world, we need to offer competitive advantages. We also need to be adaptable, build a strong network, and take big career chances.**
