### Synopsis

The book *Stop Getting in Your Own Way* (2024) talks about how not having boundaries stops us from being happy. It also shows us how to change this. Learn how saying a clear 'no' can give you new freedom. This helps you say 'yes' to yourself from the heart again.

## What You Will Learn: How to Set Boundaries and Keep Your Inner Peace.

It feels good to be open and say 'yes' to everything we meet. But sometimes, this can cause problems. If we say 'yes' too often, we can get tired. We might forget about our own well-being. 

Luckily, there is a simple answer to this problem. It is called: setting boundaries. This summary (Blink) will explain why this is important. You will learn how to stand up for yourself. This is true even if you usually try to please everyone. Discover how to set boundaries well. This will help you find new freedom and live your life your own way. Sounds good? Let's begin now.

## Blink 1 - Say 'No' to a Life Without Limits and 'Yes' to Inner Peace

Imagine a calendar full of meetings, a phone that rings all the time, and 327 unread emails. You are in the middle of all this. You try hard to keep up with everything. But the list of tasks and problems does not get shorter. Inside, you feel empty. You feel only tired, worn out, and a little bit angry at yourself and the world.

Do you know this feeling? A life without limits feels like you are always open to others' needs. People's hopes, requests, ideas, and demands come to you. And you say 'yes.' You say it again and again. Maybe you do not want to upset anyone. Maybe you learned that fitting in means being safe. Or maybe you are scared of being rejected if you do not do what others want. But you pay a high cost for this. Every 'yes' to something that is not good for you is a 'no' to yourself. Over time, these many small 'no's can make you feel helpless inside. 

This is not strange. If you do not set limits, you use all your energy on things that are outside of you. You are always busy reacting, working, and meeting what others expect. Your focus goes where other people want it. It does not go where it helps you. So, you often miss out on what you need. You get annoyed and tired more quickly. Even things you used to enjoy become less fun. This is because you feel stressed inside all the time. You cannot enjoy the moment. 'A life without limits' might sound like freedom. But it is actually the opposite. It means you are always giving up on yourself.

You will feel better when you understand this. Saying 'no' is not being mean. It is being honest. Let go of feelings of guilt. Know that you have a right to inner peace. You do not need to earn this peace. It is already yours. So, protect it.

What does this mean for your daily life? Imagine someone asks you for a favor. Your first thought is to say 'yes' right away. Before you answer, stop for a moment. Feel your body. Does it feel open or tight when you think about it? Do you feel light or under pressure? This feeling is often more honest than thinking too much. If it feels tight, your 'no' can be polite, but clear. You do not need to explain yourself or give long reasons. A simple 'That does not work for me right now' is enough. This way, you keep your energy for yourself.

A second important step is to take your own 'yes' seriously again. Make sure you find time for things that feed your soul. Maybe it is an evening on the sofa with your favorite book? Turn off your phone and laptop. Enjoy not being reached by anyone! Or, plan a regular time each week that is just for you. This could be a French class, a yoga session, or a cup of tea with a friend. The main thing is to do something for yourself. Do this even if you feel stress coming. Every time you protect this special 'me-time,' you improve your inner balance. You also learn to set boundaries.

Inner peace can spread to others. If you allow yourself to have it, you also give it to the people around you. You will see that others will value you more. This is the secret to living a life that feels right for you.

## Blink 2 - A Promise to Yourself: Name and Describe Your Boundaries.

When you know that you can protect your inner peace, your life can become calm. To stop feeling constantly busy, you need to name and describe your personal boundaries clearly.

Here is a simple but strong exercise: Think about how energy flows in your life. Ask yourself honestly: What feels heavy? What situations seem harmless but leave you tired and take your energy? Maybe it is a glass of wine at night that does not truly relax you. Maybe it is certain TV shows or social media that make you feel not good enough – not beautiful, smart, rich, thin, or successful. Or perhaps it is talks that make you feel stressed and unable to breathe. Or people who take more from you than they give back. 

The next step is to make a clear personal boundary from what you noticed. For example, say: 'I will not feel guilty when I say no. I will give my body, mind, and heart what they need.' Or: 'I will not stay in places where people always judge or gossip. This takes away my peace.' 

Write these statements down. Do not use your phone. Write them by hand on a piece of paper. Put this paper where you will often see it every day. This will help you remember the promise you made to yourself. 

Do not try to do too much at once. At first, focus on only one boundary. Choose the one that affects your life the most right now. Take one month and see what changes. If you do not feel any change, you might need to try harder. You might need a stronger boundary. After this, you can slowly add more boundaries. Even one new boundary per month is enough. It can change your life gently but strongly. 

Then comes the part that many people find scary. You must also show these boundaries to others. If someone asks you to do something that crosses your limit, a calm and kind 'no' is enough. If they still ask why, you can explain your boundary with a few short, polite words. Do not let guilt or pressure make you give away your inner peace. With some practice, this will become easier for you. You will feel less need to explain yourself. Your self-respect will grow.

## Blink 3 - The Most Important Boundary of All: Stop Negative Thoughts.

The most important boundary you can set is in your mind. What makes you truly tired and worried is usually not the situation itself. It is the thoughts you have about it.

If you do not set limits for your mind, it often goes into 'survival mode'. It looks for dangers around you. It thinks about the past. It imagines a bad future. This constant thinking goes on quietly in your mind. You often do not even notice it. And this is the problem. This quiet, negative noise is a main cause of stress, fear, and inner pain.

The key idea is this: You do not have to believe every thought you have. A thought is not real. You can choose to accept it or not. Every time a negative thought comes, you have a choice. Will you focus on it and feel bad? Or will you say 'no, thank you' and let it pass, like a cloud in the sky?

What does this mean for your daily life? If you have a thought like, 'I cannot do this anyway,' or 'This always happens to me,' then stop for a moment. Breathe deeply into your stomach a few times. This calms your body and takes you out of this harmful way of thinking. Then, ask yourself honestly: Does this thought help me right now? Does it give me clear ideas, strength, or peace? If the answer is no, then let it go.

If you are almost lost in many negative thoughts, there is a helpful saying. It is: 'Feel instead of think.' When you have a bad feeling, do not try to explain it or judge it right away. Just feel it. Where is it in your body? Does it feel tight, warm, or heavy? Stay with the feeling for a moment, without thinking about it. Do not try to understand it. Just feel it. A painful feeling that you truly feel will slowly go away by itself. You do not need to keep thinking about it.

Saying 'no' to bad thoughts does not mean you are pushing them away. It means the opposite. It means you are making space in your mind to think. These are spaces that belong to you, where anything is possible.

## Blink 4 - Use Your Free Space: Create the Life You Want.

In our society, many people think they must earn their free time. But this is not true. It might sound too good to be real, but you can have success and free time at the same time. You can have a career *and* a family. You can have money and joy in life. You can reach your goals and also have space for things that make you feel alive. Your boundaries are not a problem. They are a must-have.

Boundaries make you think in new ways. Imagine you want to earn more money. But you also set a clear rule: you will not work more than a certain number of hours. So, you must be creative. Your mind will look for better ideas, not just longer workdays. Boundaries help you work better.

So, do not only ask what you want to achieve. Also ask how you want to live while you work towards it. How should your daily life feel as you go? Free or rushed? Lively or tired? When you make a goal, add a boundary to protect your happiness. For example: 'I will work towards this goal, but I will not give up my weekends.' This clear thinking changes what you do and how you feel inside.

Many people work very hard for a goal. They think they will be happy only when they reach it. But real life does not happen later. It happens now. When you set boundaries for your goals, you stop waiting for joy. You bring joy into your life right now. You let the journey be the goal. In this way, you grow stronger.

Do you sometimes hear a voice inside you? Does it ask for more success, more joy, or more money? This is a sign that you need a new boundary. A boundary that makes space for the life you dream of.

## Blink 5 - Let Go and Adjust: Allow Your Boundaries to Change.

Boundaries are not like strong walls. They are like living lines. They are there to keep you safe, not to limit you. Your life is always changing. So, your boundaries can also change. Something that helped you before might now hold you back without you knowing. This is not because it was wrong. It is because you have grown.

Maybe at one time, you spent less time with people. You had fewer plans. This was to find peace or heal something inside you. That boundary was perfect at that time. But later, you might notice it no longer helps you. Instead, it stops you from moving forward. You want more closeness, more talking, and more life. If you keep old rules too strictly, caring for yourself becomes limiting yourself. To grow, you must always move. This means you sometimes need to let go of old boundaries.

To let go does not mean you are not disciplined. It does not mean you change everything all the time. It means you stay aware. You feel if a boundary still makes you strong today. Or if it now takes your energy away. Sometimes it is like a pair of shoes. They helped you for a long time. But then they start to hurt your feet. Then it is time to take them off. It is that simple.

A first step could be to loosen a boundary a little. Do not just remove it completely. For example, you might have put your job first for a long time. Now you see your most important things are changing. You could start to set regular times for your family, your friends, or yourself. See how this makes you feel. Do you have more energy? Do you feel more connected? Then you know you are making the right changes.

You need courage for such changes. So, speak kindly to yourself. Say: 'What helped me before does not have to help me now. I can choose again.' This is not turning against your past self. It shows respect for who you are today.

Boundaries are tools. They are not who you are. They are there to show your true self. If you learn to check and change them often, you will stay close to what you truly need. And this is where a life starts. A life that feels safe, but also lively and real.
