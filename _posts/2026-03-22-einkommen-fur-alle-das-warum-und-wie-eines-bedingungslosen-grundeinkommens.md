*Author: **Götz W. Werner***

_Götz W. Werner_

Reading time: 23 minutes

### Synopsis

Götz W. Werner supports a Universal Basic Income. He believes it gives everyone a chance to be part of a good society. They can also contribute to it. This is true even after the industrial age. People would not need to rely on money from the government, like unemployment benefits. In his book *Einkommen für alle *(2008), he explains why this is possible. He also shows how it could work in real life.

## What You Will Learn: Why We Should Change Our Thinking and Create a Fairer Society

A Universal Basic Income in Germany is not just a nice dream. This idea can actually work. It is also the only logical way forward for our society.

Factories are becoming more efficient. So, there will be less work in the future. We cannot solve this problem forever with unemployment benefits. But we can solve it with a basic income.

However, if we want a basic income, politicians and businesses must change their way of thinking. First, we need to think again about how we see work. We should support jobs that machines cannot replace. We should also support jobs that machines cannot make more efficient.

Second, we need big changes in our tax and money rules. These changes are needed to make a Universal Basic Income possible.

This would not mean that most people sit at home doing nothing and get paid. It would be the opposite. It would create a society where everyone uses their skills to do valuable work. And they would be paid fairly for it.

Götz W. Werner explores this idea by asking key questions. He asks why we should rethink our view of paid work. He also asks how we could build a fairer society. Finally, he looks at what steps are needed to make Universal Basic Income real. His main answers to these questions are in the next sections.

You will also learn:

- why a Universal Basic Income is part of our country's constitution,

- how taxes can be fair, and

- what 'bud damage' means.

## Blink 1 - Full Employment Is a Dream We Must Let Go Of.

The German economy has grown steadily in recent decades. But full employment has never truly been reached.

Work processes are becoming more efficient. This means fewer workers are needed. At the same time, the economy has not grown enough. It has not created new jobs.

Making products used to take a lot of effort and human work. But in recent decades, things have changed. Better work methods, new production ways, and machines have made it much more efficient. So, fewer workers are needed to make the same amount of products.

For example, imagine 100 workers make 1 million euros worth of goods in one year. The next year, 99 workers can make 1.05 million euros worth of goods. They do this by using better methods. This means productivity has gone up, even with fewer workers.

Machines are also taking over more and more human jobs. Today, when a car is made, we do not need hundreds of mechanics. They used to cast parts, weld the frame, and paint the body. Now, often just one person checks the process. Robots do the rest of the work.

To make up for these lost jobs and create new ones, industrial countries like Germany need steady economic growth of more than 3% over a long time.

But from 1992 to 2006, Germany's growth was only 1.4% on average. This was 'jobless growth'. It came from existing jobs becoming more productive. It did not come from creating new jobs.

So, full employment is a goal we cannot reach. We must change how we think about paid work.

We urgently need to change. The unfair way goods are shared in our society shows this.

## Blink 2 - Germany Has Many Goods, But Not Everyone Can Afford Them.

Waiting two years for a car is a thing of the past. Germany produces many goods. These range from chocolate bars and drills to plasma TVs. We have more than enough of everything. We live in a society of plenty. In theory, there is more than enough for everyone. But only if you have enough money.

We can see that there are too many goods in our shops. Think back to Germany's reunification. When the Berlin Wall fell, West Germany had enough goods. It could meet all the needs of East Germany overnight.

So, enough goods are made for everyone. But it is wrong to think that everyone has the same access to these goods. Only about 40 million Germans have paid jobs. Only about one-third of Germans pay into social insurance.

This means many people have little money, even though there are plenty of goods. Money is not shared fairly. The richest 10% of Germans own over 40% of the country's wealth. But the poorest 50% of Germans only own about 5%. Some people even look for empty bottles in bins to get by each day.

These people can only dream of luxury goods. These goods are often made scarce and expensive. This is so only a few rich people can buy them. When someone sells luxury fashion, they are not really selling a product. They are selling a special right. This creates a false idea that there is not enough.

Germany produces enough goods so that everyone should have what they need. But only a few Germans can afford these many goods. This is because money is not shared equally.

## Blink 3 - Making Things Is Not Everything: It Only Meets Our Basic Needs.

Article 1 of our Basic Law says that human dignity is very important. This means we must be able to meet our basic needs. For example, we need food so we don't starve. We also need a home so we don't get cold. Making goods meets these needs.

But living a good life also means meeting our other needs. These are needs like talking to other people. It means taking part in society and cultural events.

Just making lots of goods does not make people happy. It does not meet all human needs. Our needs for ideas, beauty, art, knowledge, or spirit cannot be met by owning or buying things.

To live with dignity, a person needs to take part in political, social, and cultural life. Meeting these social and cultural needs makes us truly human.

Teachers, for example, do this kind of work. A teacher does not make physical goods. Instead, they share values and knowledge. So, a teacher does not produce things, but they help society in an important way.

People can live if only their basic needs are met. But to protect the basic rights of our citizens, we need to do more. We must focus on social and cultural work, not just making goods.

A Universal Basic Income is the answer.

## Blink 4 - Universal Basic Income Is a Basic Right in Our Constitution.

Many people wrongly think that the right to *work* is in our basic laws. But the truth is, everyone has the right to a fair income. This right comes from our basic laws. To live freely and with dignity, we need a fair income. This income should not be linked to a certain job.

However, the current law says you must take any job the employment office offers. You have to do this to avoid having no money. This is true even if you are too skilled for the job. This is like forced labor. Article 12 of our Basic Law actually forbids it.

So, people have a right to income, not a right to work. Our basic rights do not depend on if we work. They come from the fact that we are simply here on Earth.

But in our society, we need money to get food, clothes, education, and social activities. Everyone should get this money. This is part of their basic rights. It should not matter if they work or not.

So, a Universal Basic Income is a very important part of our rights as citizens. The right to live and grow old freely means you can say no to a job. You can still be financially safe.

A Universal Basic Income is a right to freedom in our constitution. We live in a society with many goods. So, we can and must accept this right.

This also means some jobs should pay more. Especially jobs that do not involve making goods.

## Blink 5 - Cultural and Social Work Is 'Real' Work and Should Be Paid Fairly.

In our society, we value making things much more than cultural and social work. An engineer in the car industry earns a very good salary. But a care worker for the elderly is lucky if they don't need extra money from the government (Hartz IV).

The results of making things are becoming less important. We already make too many products. Instead, our society is now focusing more on other topics. These include education, helping young people, and caring for the growing number of older people.

But work that helps other people is often not seen as 'real' work. For example, housework or caring for family and children is not paid. People don't see it as a normal job. Yet, it is vital for society to work well.

We need to pay more attention to social and cultural jobs. We must create better working conditions in these areas. This work meets our social needs. It is becoming more and more important for society.

It makes no sense to try to make cultural work faster, cheaper, or more productive. This is different from making goods. Cultural work is not about speed or saving money. It is about human care and attention.

For example, a nursing home can save money by having fewer staff. From a business point of view, this seems like a good idea. But it means that patients get less time and care.

If these jobs paid well, cultural work would become more attractive. Our society really needs more people in this area. Think about crowded classrooms or public places that owe money.

When there were not enough goods, it made sense to pay production work well. But now we have many goods. So, it is time to change our way of thinking.

This leads to a bold idea: All work should be seen as equally important and paid fairly. What would happen then?

## Blink 6 - With a Basic Income, Everyone Takes Responsibility in Society.

No one can make all the goods and services they need by themselves. Everyone depends on others' work. We rely on farmers for food, doctors for healing, and actors for entertainment. So, we must all do our best. This way, we can have bread for breakfast and get help for toothaches.

Some people might think that a guaranteed monthly payment will not make people work hard. They might think people won't try their best. But it can work. The best way is to create a society where everyone lives together. This society must believe that each person knows their duty. And because of this, they will do their best.

We must trust that people want to get better and do good work. Always watching people only makes them feel trapped and forced.

Also, we must learn to put our own needs aside for this society to work. Now, many people want the government to support them. But they don't want to pay to support others. This is why many people avoid paying taxes.

This creates a bad cycle. The more people avoid paying into the common fund, the less money there is for everyone's safety. So, more people try to get by without paying. Then the government has to raise more taxes to fill the gap.

But if everyone had a basic income, this cycle could stop. Everyone would do their best. They would know that others would support them when times are hard.

This would also stop people from saving too much. That habit slows down our economy.

## Blink 7 - Saving Money Stops New Value. Profits Must Be Reinvested to Improve Goods.

You don't need to be an expert to know that when people buy more, the economy grows. It's simple: more spending means more money moving around.

This money means investments for businesses. A company can only invest in new products if it gets enough money from the market. The problem in Germany right now is that households save too much. This hurts the economy and makes it weaker.

A company can only grow if it invests its profits. The more profit it invests, the more money it has for research and new ideas. New ideas only come from research and development.

Think of a farmer planting crops. If the farmer eats all the food from the harvest, he will live well for a short time. But he will have no seeds for the next harvest. So, he will go hungry next year. The more he plants new seeds from each harvest, the better off he will be in the long run.

People want to feel safe with their money, and that's understandable. But saved money is just 'parked'. It cannot be used. This stops the process of creating value. Money itself has no value. If nothing is produced anymore, money cannot buy you anything.

With a basic income, people would not need to save so much. They would know they always have enough to live. So, money would not be stored away for no reason. We could spend it more freely. Money that is now 'frozen' in savings could be used in a basic income society. It could be put into education, support programs, or science for the future.

But it's not just up to citizens to help the economy grow. The government must also do its part. For example, it could change tax laws.

## Blink 8 - Company Taxes Should Be Removed to Help Germany's Economy.

Companies need money to make the goods our society uses. But this money is not always safe. If people don't buy a company's products, its money runs out. Then the company leaves the market.

If the government taxes a company before it even makes goods or services, this hurts the company. It stops the company from making good products at low prices for customers.

The problem is this: Money put into making things is an investment for the future. So, taxing companies actually hurts society. The goods they make might not be as good as they could be if there were no taxes.

If you tax an apple grower, that is *bud damage*. You are not hurting the farmer. You are stopping him from giving good apples to society. This makes it harder to grow many good apples at a low price for everyone.

Another reason to stop taxing companies is that Germany's company taxes are higher than in many other countries. This affects business decisions. Companies sometimes move all or part of their production out of Germany. This means big losses for the German economy.

If there were no taxes on companies, they could make choices based on other things. Then, many companies might stay in Germany. For example, they would invest where workers are very productive. Or they would invest where products fit the local market best.

Company taxes harm production. They also make prices wrong. Removing them would help German businesses and make the economy stronger.

All current taxes should be removed. They should be replaced by a consumption tax. This is the only useful tax.

## Blink 9 - A Fair Consumption Tax Is the Only Just Tax.

At first, Germany's income tax system seems fair. It has a tax-free amount and a high tax rate for high earners. But this is not completely true. The richer a German citizen is, the more taxes they avoid. Poor people have to pay more as a result.

The more money a person has, the easier it is to avoid income tax. Rich people should pay high taxes. But often, they put their money in bank accounts in other countries. Or they register their main home in places like Switzerland or Monaco, where taxes are lower.

But with a fair consumption tax, the tax burden would be shared fairly. With only a consumption tax, everyone would pay tax on what they buy. There would be no way to avoid paying taxes.

Currently, Germany has different sales tax rates, like 7% or 19%. In a society with only a consumption tax, we could make these rates even more detailed. For example, luxury items would be more expensive. This would mean rich people pay more tax than poor people. Unlike now, a consumption tax would make those who buy more or more expensive goods and services pay more.

With this plan, basic foods would have a very low tax rate. The more luxury an item is, the higher the tax rate would be. For example, basic foods could have a 3% tax. Other foods and treats could have 10%. Special items like truffles or caviar could have 80%. A powerful private car could also be taxed four times more than a small car.

If there were only a consumption tax, rich people could no longer avoid paying government fees. With a tax that gets higher for more expensive items, people with more money would pay more tax. The tax system would be simpler and fairer.

All of society would benefit from this.

## Summary

The main message of this book is:

**Everyone has the right to be part of society. But this can only happen with a Universal Basic Income. It also needs a change in how we think as a society. And we need big tax changes. These changes should tax what people buy, not companies.**

What you can do:

**Trust other people.**

A society works better when everyone trusts that others want to help. This is better than a society where lawyers complain about factory workers, and factory workers complain about jobless people.

**Value work that helps people. **

Social work, care jobs, and volunteer efforts are all important for a good society. Everyone should value them. Work that makes money is not the only important work!
