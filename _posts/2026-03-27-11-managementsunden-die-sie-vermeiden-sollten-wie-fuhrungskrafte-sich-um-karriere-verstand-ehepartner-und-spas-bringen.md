### Synopsis

The book *11 Management Sins You Should Avoid* (2009) is about how to manage well. It helps you do this without getting too stressed. These summaries show you how to enjoy your job instead. You will learn to remove the usual risks that cause management mistakes.

## What you will learn: How to avoid the most dangerous problems as a manager.

What makes a great manager? Is it a very sharp mind? Is it amazing charm? Or is good management just about using common sense? And about avoiding the biggest mistakes?

Managers can make mistakes everywhere. For example, they might avoid making decisions. Or they might not listen to important advice. They might work alone instead of asking for other opinions. Some managers are afraid of making a mistake. Others act too quickly and cause many problems. Then it is very hard to fix these problems.

Other leaders fail when they try to motivate their staff. They do not follow their own strategy. They treat staff like they are all the same. They do not care about each person’s needs. They do not treat staff with respect. Some managers think they must solve every problem themselves. They work too much instead of building a good team and sharing tasks.

These summaries of *11 Management Sins You Should Avoid* show how to avoid these mistakes. They show how managers can lead successfully and enjoy their work.

In these summaries, you will also learn:

- why it is good to have a discussion partner when making decisions,

- how to prevent yourself from acting too fast as a manager, and

- why a team of only competitive people will fail.

## Blink 1 - Do not run away from decisions.

It is tempting for any manager to delay important decisions. For example, about expanding into Eastern Europe or investing in a new product. If you do not decide, you cannot make a wrong decision. But waiting like this often stops the company from growing. It also stops the manager’s career from moving forward.

So, instead of not deciding, you should try hard to make the *right* decision. To decide correctly, think about all possible risks. Check them, reduce them, or remove them if you can. Then take a small, well-planned risk. For this risk, you should also have a backup plan ready to use if something goes wrong.

Imagine you have a new big client, but they do not want to pay upfront. You can try to find a middle ground. Maybe the client will pay half in advance. Then, if they do not pay the rest, you can still cover it with money from your other clients.

Think about the worst thing that could happen with your decision. How could you stop it or fix it? Make a decision, but do not do it without thinking. Choose a balance between waiting too long and deciding too fast.

You should also think about the costs of not deciding. Or of deciding differently. Maybe your competitor would be the first in Eastern Europe. Or your company might seem old-fashioned because it has not had new products for years.

## Blink 2 - Let outsiders and your gut feeling help you with decisions.

It is not easy to make the right decisions. After you have looked at all options for a while, you reach a point. More analysis will not give you new ideas. At this point, you should stop analyzing. Start getting more support through feedback.

Ask for other people's opinions. People from outside give feedback without being too much influenced by "facts." Have you ever seen a financial plan that shows bad results? Even with good numbers, it is often useful to get an honest opinion from an outsider. Then, you might even say no, if needed.

When you must make decisions fast, do one of two things. If you have some time, ask everyone you can for feedback. If time is very short, trust your gut feeling. Brain research shows that gut feelings are often better in some situations, especially when you are in a hurry. Questions like "Would I put my own money on this decision?" can help you make a quick decision based on your intuition.

In the end, as a manager, you must decide alone and take responsibility. But a sparring partner can make the decision process better beforehand. This is someone you fully trust. You can openly share ideas and discuss different arguments with them. It is important that your sparring partner is careful and honest. This way, they can stop you from making wrong decisions if needed.

## Blink 3 - Balance acting and thinking.

Some managers are like Lucky Luke, acting faster than they can think. They send emails that say different things to different staff. They give orders without thinking about what will happen. They do not care about the money needed for their big ideas. They also do not think about how their quick actions might affect clients, staff, or their boss. They do not even check if they can manage the project they started.

It is actually not hard to guide the energy of such fast-acting managers in a good way. What these managers need to learn is to pause and think before they act. They should ask themselves: "How will this affect different people? What will it really achieve?"

The fear of failing and the pressure to succeed often cause leaders to rush. But this does not work in the long run. If you make your staff too busy with your quick actions, you harm yourself.

However, if you think before you speak, you help your staff. Then, they do not have to work for days on a plan that will later be thrown away. This happens because the plan was not thought through from the start. Staff who can trust their boss’s orders usually respect them. But staff often laugh at a fast-acting manager behind their back.

A good balance of thinking and acting is also good for teamwork. As a leader, create the right reasons for action if you see too much or too little thinking. For those who always wait, you can take away their fear of wrong decisions. Do this by taking responsibility yourself. If you truly show you support the project, and even if it fails, your staff will be unstoppable. For those who act without thinking, stop giving them money. Do this until they show you a good project plan.

## Blink 4 - Live your company's strategy.

A company's strategy is key to its success. But even though it is very important, it is often not shared beyond the top leaders. It sometimes does not reach every employee. How can the sales team use a new plan, like getting more new customers? They cannot if they do not know about the great new ideas from the management.

If you want your strategy to reach every employee, you must live it. You must show it in everything you do. You must talk about it as much as you can. Repeating it is also good. Especially if sales have focused on happy loyal customers for years. It is hard to change to a strategy that aims to get more new customers.

The strategy must be clear. This way, each employee knows what is expected of them. No one will figure out what specific actions are hidden behind fancy words or empty phrases. Your job as a leader is to explain this. You also need to be a good example.

It is important that you make sure the strategy is followed. If staff act against the strategy – for example, they do not try to get new customers but only please old ones, and get away with it – they will not change. You must make sure that all staff support your company's strategy. And that it shows in everything they do.

If many people are part of deciding on a new strategy early on, there will be less resistance later. People from the lower parts of the company who do the actual work can also see real weaknesses in the strategy much better. For example, getting new customers might not be possible. This could be because only loyal customers are willing to pay the company's high prices.

## Blink 5 - Push the right buttons for each employee.

One of a manager's jobs is to motivate their staff. It is not hard if you know how, but most managers seem not to. Do you feel your boss motivates you? And let’s be honest: do you motivate your staff as well as you can?

To keep staff motivated, it is important to do more than just share information. You need to understand each employee and their personality. Instead of just asking, "Why is this taking so long again?" you should explain that you need the results quickly. Involve the employee in solving the problem and thank them for their effort.

Of course, motivated staff can also bring some risk: they tend to speak their minds. But also, being able to speak freely without punishment is very motivating. So, choose the smaller problem. Choose to have motivated staff. Accept it if they sometimes have a different opinion than you. Do not get angry. Instead, listen to their criticism and use it. Usually, it will be fair, or have some truth in it. Trust the good ideas of your motivated staff. Do not reject them because of false pride.

It also inspires your staff if you listen to them carefully. Your staff will feel heard. And it helps you get to know them better. This is a valuable thing for your company. Every person is different. This means you should find and push each employee’s unique motivation button. One person needs many details in a task because they cannot work with general instructions. The next person might be a free spirit. They need general instructions because it gives them freedom to create. Speak to each employee's personal reasons for doing things.

## Blink 6 - Only do what you are paid for: manage.

Many managers stay in the office late into the evening or night. All other staff have already gone home. Does this make them good managers? No, it is the opposite. They do not trust their staff. They check everything, even small bills. They sit in every meeting, even unimportant ones, and waste their time. They cannot say no when a seemingly helpless employee asks for help.

A manager’s only job is to manage. This means making decisions and planning for the future. You should leave everything else to your staff. Your company will work better if you make sure your staff can manage without you. Your job is not to fix all the mistakes others make. Instead, you should make sure these mistakes happen as little as possible.

Some staff learn to act helpless over time. They try to do as little work as possible. They give a lot of their tasks to the boss. Do not try to be the company's hero. Instead, help the employee solve the problem themselves. Ask specific questions like: "What do you suggest to solve this problem?"

It is very important that you do not miss important decisions. This happens if you are too busy with daily tasks. Leave the everyday work to your staff. To do this, sort all tasks into "important" and "urgent." Things that are only urgent should be done by someone else.

Working until you are totally tired is also not helpful. You cannot move your company forward in the long run this way. The head of development for a machine builder was once caught by a board member in his office. He was sitting with his feet up, looking dreamily out the window. The board member could hardly believe it. But then he heard the strategic market analysis from the development head. He had thought of it in that relaxed position. The board member's only comment was: "Keep your feet where they are until you have a solution!"

## Blink 7 - Match your expectations with those of the people you talk to.

In personal relationships, misunderstandings usually only cause small problems. But in work, they often cost a lot of money. If the boss shouts, "Please give Mr. Schmitz priority this time!" as he leaves, he should not be surprised. His staff might then slow down important projects for Mr. Schmitz. This could happen even if he only wanted to stop Mr. Schmitz's order from being delayed for two weeks again.

To be understood, it is best to assume you will not be understood. This makes you communicate clearly, precisely, and fully enough. You are responsible for making sure what you want to say is understood correctly. To check this, ask questions. Make sure the task was really understood. Share your expectations clearly. This might seem like a waste of time at first. But it saves time in the long run. It avoids having to do the task two or three times if your staff misunderstood it.

Expectations must also be shared the other way around. Your boss might also find it hard to say clearly what he expects. So, next time you think you are doing what your boss wants, think carefully. Was it really what he meant? If you are unsure, ask. This will match your understanding of the task with your boss's goal. For example, he might mostly expect you to reach your performance goals. And only secondarily, that you spend your time dancing at parties to represent your company as managing director.

## Blink 8 - Build teams with different kinds of people.

For women managers, their careers often stop at a certain level. An invisible barrier stops them from becoming more than, for example, a department head. This happens no matter how good they are.

Mistakes made by women managers are also punished much more strictly. Unlike their male colleagues, who are supported by an "old boys' network," women are laughed at for mistakes. They rarely get a similar job again.

Such unfair treatment, which can also happen to other groups, shows a company’s poor social skills. In the long run, it harms those who cause it, like a boomerang. This creates a team of similar people. This slow, uniform group has little chance of creating new ideas or making important changes.

Teams with many different kinds of people, who work well together, greatly increase a company's success. Their members help each other, challenge each other, and inspire new ideas. That is why it is good to bring together people with very different skills, attitudes, and backgrounds. But because there are more differences, these mixed teams need very skilled leadership. This leadership must guide the different ideas in a positive way.

## Blink 9 - Choose staff yourself and look at their character.

Many managers think that hiring new staff is only the HR department's job. HR chooses staff by looking at resumes and finding the most skilled people. If the result is completely wrong – either the new employee does not like the job and leaves quickly, or their boss thinks they are an idiot – many managers believe HR messed up again.

But with this attitude, you make it too easy for yourself. It is important for you to get and keep the best and most suitable staff. Otherwise, you will constantly train new staff or settle for less skilled people. Make sure HR gets a job description from you. This way, the basic requirements match what you want. It is also very important that you, as the direct boss, are involved in all other steps of choosing staff.

Keep an eye on good candidates yourself, because HR departments are always very busy. So do not just rely on HR to stay in touch. Take care of it yourself! Good people are quickly hired by others.

It is best to take part in the job interview. Only then can you judge if the candidate has the right qualities for your team. You can see if they will fit in well. Their technical skills are less important. You can always train your new employee. But you will hardly be able to change their character.

But do not fall into the "liking" trap. Just because someone has the same hobbies as you, or agrees with everything you say, does not mean they will make the team better. Imagine working with a team full of competitive marathon runners. Other important things for team success, like planning and creating, would probably be missed in such a team.

## Blink 10 - Do not let yourself be blackmailed and stay honest.

There are times when managers face strong pressure to act dishonestly. For example, their career might depend on reaching a sales goal. Or a key customer might ask for a bribe and say they could give their business to a competitor. This is basically blackmail: give money or lose the job.

The big question is: do you have to give in to blackmail? The answer is no. Even if the other person only offers two choices, there are always third and fourth options. In such cases, do not rely on money for favors. Rely on your good relationships. To build these, you should create a network based on friendships, not bribes.

A tougher tactic is to turn the tables. You can blackmail the blackmailer back. How would they feel if their boss found out they give contracts based on personal gain, not on quality? This tactic needs a lot of self-confidence. So, it is not always recommended.

It is easy to be dishonest, because it is the path of least resistance. But good managers do not need to do this. Also, while it is easier in the short term to give and take bribes, it usually does not pay off in the long run.

Only truly honest people can be trusted. Do it for yourself! Otherwise, your self-confidence and conscience will suffer in the long run. If you are dishonest yourself, others in your company will soon copy you. And that is the beginning of the end.

## Blink 11 - Enjoy your work.

Not only your staff need to be motivated, but you do too. If you want to feel happy with your job in the long term, you must make it as pleasant as possible. And how can you inspire your staff if you are already tired and unmotivated?

Of course, you usually cannot decide exactly what your work will be. So, find small projects that are exactly what you have always wanted to do. This could be developing a new product. Or anything else that fits your personal interests. Find out what you enjoy, and then do it, at least in a small way.

The part of your work that feels like a duty, you should do in a way that gives you the most joy. This will make you much more productive. A business consultant, who you might expect to analyze huge piles of spreadsheets, can instead visit different branches. They can talk to people there about what problems might be causing issues. In the end, what matters is the result, not how you work.

People who enjoy their work are successful. So find out what is most important to you personally. Then, let that guide your professional actions. For example, a security company owner always promised complete security. He won against his competitors in the long run, even with much higher prices. He enjoyed his work, while the competitors gradually gave up because they had problems among themselves.

## Summary

A good manager does not need special skills. They just need to cleverly avoid the usual traps of daily management. If you follow a few simple rules, you will be successful and enjoy your job in the long run.
